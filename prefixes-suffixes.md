## 1.1 Prefixes and Suffixes

Prefixes are added to the beginning of a word. They usually change the meaning of the word but rarely change its grammatical category (e.g., a noun usually remains a noun). Suffixes are added to the end of a word and often change both meaning and grammatical category (e.g., a verb can become a noun or adjective).

---

### A. Prefixes

#### 1. Negation / Opposites

| Prefix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `un-` | not | unhappy, unable, uncertain |
| `in-` / `im-` / `il-` / `ir-` | not | invisible, impossible, illegal, irregular |
| `dis-` | not, opposite of | disagree, disappear, dishonest |
| `non-` | not | nonsense, nonfiction, nonstop |

#### 2. Time / Order

| Prefix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `re-` | again, back | redo, reread, return, review |
| `pre-` | before | preview, prefix, prehistoric |
| `post-` | after | postgraduate, postscript (P.S.) |

#### 3. Place / Location

| Prefix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `inter-` | between, among | international, interstate, interview |
| `sub-` | under, below | submarine, subway, subject |
| `hyper-` | over, above, beyond, excessively | hyperactive, hypercritical |
| `super-` | above, beyond | supermarket, supervise, supernatural |
| `trans-` | across, beyond | transport, transatlantic |

#### 4. Number / Quantity

| Prefix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `uni-` | one | unicycle, uniform |
| `bi-` | two | bicycle, bilingual |
| `tri-` | three | tricycle, trilogy |
| `multi-` | many | multitasking, multiple |

#### 5. Degree / Size

| Prefix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `mini-` | small | miniskirt, minibus |
| `maxi-` | large | maxidress |

---

### B. Suffixes

Suffixes are added to the end of a word. They can change the meaning of a word, but more often, they change the word's grammatical type (part of speech) - for example, turning a verb into a noun, or an adjective into an adverb.

---

#### 1. Noun-Forming Suffixes (making nouns)

| Suffix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `-ness` | state of being | happiness, kindness, softness (from adjectives) |
| `-ity` / `-ty` | state or quality of being | activity, ability, safety (from adjectives) |
| `-ment` | action or result | agreement, government, development (from verbs) |
| `-tion` / `-sion` | action or state | education, expression, conclusion (from verbs) |
| `-er` / `-or` | person or thing that does something | teacher, farmer, actor, computer (from verbs) |
| `-ist` | person who believes in or practices something | artist, scientist, economist |
| `-ism` | belief, practice, doctrine | socialism, heroism, criticism |

---

#### 2. Adjective-Forming Suffixes (making adjectives)

| Suffix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `-able` / `-ible` | capable of being | comfortable, accessible, visible (from nouns/verbs) |
| `-ful` | full of | wonderful, beautiful, helpful (from nouns) |
| `-less` | without | hopeless, fearless, endless (from nouns) |
| `-ous` / `-ious` | having the quality of | dangerous, famous, delicious (from nouns) |
| `-ic` / `-al` | relating to | musical, economic, national (from nouns) |
| `-y` | characterized by | dirty, funny, healthy (from nouns) |

---

#### 3. Verb-Forming Suffixes (making verbs)

| Suffix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `-ize` / `-ise` | to make or become | modernize, recognize, realise (from nouns/adjectives) |
| `-en` | to make or become | soften, broaden, strengthen (from adjectives) |

---

#### 4. Adverb-Forming Suffixes (making adverbs)

| Suffix | Meaning | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `-ly` | in a certain manner (most common) | quickly, slowly, beautifully (from adjectives) |
| `-ward` / `-wards` | direction | toward(s), backward(s), upward(s) |

### C. Combining Prefixes and Suffixes

Often, words can have both a prefix and a suffix. The prefix typically modifies the meaning, while the suffix often changes the word type (part of speech).

| Base Word | Prefix + Base + Suffix | Result | Explanation |
|-----------|------------------------|--------|-------------|
| happy | `un-` + happy + `-ness` | **unhappiness** | prefix changes meaning (not happy), suffix changes to noun (state of being) |
| act | `re-` + act + `-ion` | **reaction** | prefix changes meaning (again/back), suffix changes to noun (action or state) |
| vis (see) | `in-` + vis + `-ible` | **invisible** | prefix changes meaning (not), suffix changes to adjective (capable of being) |

**More examples:**

| Base | With Prefix and Suffix | Meaning |
|------|------------------------|---------|
| care | `un-` + care + `-ing` = **uncaring** | not showing care |
| hope | `hope` + `-less` + `-ness` = **hopelessness** | the state of being without hope |
| agree | `dis-` + agree + `-ment` = **disagreement** | the state of not agreeing |
| understand | `mis-` + understand + `-ing` = **misunderstanding** | the act of understanding incorrectly |
| possible | `im-` + possible + `-ity` = **impossibility** | the quality of not being possible |
| employ | `un-` + employ + `-ment` = **unemployment** | the state of not being employed |

> **Note:** When adding suffixes, spelling changes may occur (e.g., happy → happiness, replacing 'y' with 'i'). When adding prefixes, the base word usually remains unchanged.
