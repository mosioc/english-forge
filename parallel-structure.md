## 23 Parallel Structure (Parallelism)

Parallel structure (also called parallelism) means using the same grammatical form for items in a list, comparison, or series. It makes sentences clearer, more balanced, and easier to read. When elements are not parallel, the sentence becomes awkward and confusing.

> **Related Sections:** See *[Section 20. Conjunctions](./conjunctions.md)* for connecting elements and *[Section 19.1 Order of Adjectives](./order-of-adjectives.md)* for adjective order.

---

### A. What Is Parallel Structure?

Parallel structure requires that words, phrases, or clauses in a series have the **same grammatical form**.

| Not Parallel (Awkward) | Parallel (Correct) |
|------------------------|--------------------|
| *She likes **running**, **to swim**, and **bicycles**.* | *She likes **running**, **swimming**, and **bicycling**.* |
| *He is **smart**, **hardworking**, and has **charm**.* | *He is **smart**, **hardworking**, and **charming**.* |
| *I came, **saw**, and **was conquering**.* | *I **came**, **saw**, and **conquered**.* |

---

### B. Parallel Structure with Lists and Series

When listing three or more items, each item must have the same grammatical form.

#### 1. Nouns with Nouns

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *She bought **apples**, **bananas**, and **some oranges**.* | *She bought **apples**, **bananas**, and **oranges**.* |
| *His hobbies include **reading**, **painting**, and **to run**.* | *His hobbies include **reading**, **painting**, and **running**.* |

#### 2. Adjectives with Adjectives

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *The movie was **exciting**, **funny**, and a **thrill**.* | *The movie was **exciting**, **funny**, and **thrilling**.* |
| *She is **kind**, **intelligent**, and has **beauty**.* | *She is **kind**, **intelligent**, and **beautiful**.* |

#### 3. Verbs with Verbs

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *He **woke up**, **ate** breakfast, and was **leaving**.* | *He **woke up**, **ate** breakfast, and **left**.* |
| *I **need** to study, **to practice**, and **working** hard.* | *I **need to study**, **to practice**, and **to work** hard.* |

#### 4. Adverbs with Adverbs

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *She spoke **quickly**, **clearly**, and with **confidence**.* | *She spoke **quickly**, **clearly**, and **confidently**.* |

#### 5. Phrases with Phrases

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *He is known for **his honesty** and **being kind**.* | *He is known for **his honesty** and **his kindness**.* |
| *The candidate has **experience**, **a degree**, and is **motivated**.* | *The candidate has **experience**, **a degree**, and **motivation**.* |

#### 6. Clauses with Clauses

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *I forgot **that I had a meeting** and **to call my boss**.* | *I forgot **that I had a meeting** and **that I needed to call my boss**.* |

---

### C. Parallel Structure with Correlative Conjunctions

Correlative conjunctions are pairs of conjunctions that connect equal elements. The grammatical form on both sides of the pair must be parallel.

**Common Correlative Conjunctions:**

| Pair | Example |
|------|---------|
| *both... and...* | *She is **both** intelligent **and** hardworking.* |
| *either... or...* | *You can **either** stay **or** go.* |
| *neither... nor...* | *He is **neither** rich **nor** famous.* |
| *not only... but also...* | *She **not only** sings **but also** dances.* |
| *whether... or...* | *I don't know **whether** to laugh **or** cry.* |
| *as... as...* | *He is **as** tall **as** his brother.* |

#### Examples:

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *She is **both** smart **and** has kindness.* | *She is **both** smart **and** kind.* |
| *He **not only** plays guitar **but also** singing.* | *He **not only** plays guitar **but also** sings.* |
| *You can **either** call me **or** sending an email.* | *You can **either** call me **or** send an email.* |
| *I like **neither** to run **nor** swimming.* | *I like **neither** to run **nor** to swim.* / *I like **neither** running **nor** swimming.* |

---

### D. Parallel Structure with Comparisons (*than* and *as*)

When making comparisons, the items being compared must have the same grammatical form.

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *His singing is better than **to dance**.* | *His singing is better than **his dancing**.* |
| *She runs as fast as **a professional runner does**.* | *She runs as fast as **a professional runner**.* |
| *I find reading more enjoyable than **to watch TV**.* | *I find reading more enjoyable than **watching TV**.* |

---

### E. Parallel Structure with Lists After a Colon

When a colon introduces a list, each item in the list must be parallel.

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *He needs three things: **money**, **time**, and **to be motivated**.* | *He needs three things: **money**, **time**, and **motivation**.* |
| *Her duties include: **answering** phones, **filing** papers, and **to greet** clients.* | *Her duties include: **answering** phones, **filing** papers, and **greeting** clients.* |

---

### F. Parallel Structure in Outlines and Headings

In formal writing (reports, presentations, résumés), parallel structure is essential for headings and bullet points.

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *Responsibilities: **Managing** staff, **budget** planning, and **to train** new employees.* | *Responsibilities: **Managing** staff, **planning** budgets, and **training** new employees.* |
| *Experience: **Worked** as cashier, **customer service** skills, **supervised** team.* | *Experience: **Worked** as cashier, **developed** customer service skills, **supervised** team.* |

---

### G. Parallel Structure with Verbs After Auxiliaries

When using auxiliary verbs, the main verbs should maintain parallel form.

| Not Parallel | Parallel |
|--------------|----------|
| *I **have** eaten and **going** to sleep.* | *I **have** eaten and **am going** to sleep.* |
| *She **can** swim and **running** fast.* | *She **can** swim and **run** fast.* |
| *He **was** tired and **needed** to rest.* | *He **was** tired and **needed** to rest.* (already parallel - both past tense) |

---

### H. Parallel Structure in Formal vs. Informal English

| Context | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Formal (Requires strict parallelism)** | *The proposal is cost-effective, efficient, and innovative.* |
| **Informal (Parallelism often relaxed)** | *He's nice, smart, and has a good sense of humor.* (Informal, but still better to parallelize) |

> **Tip:** Even in informal English, parallelism makes you sound more polished and professional.

---

### I. Common Errors and Corrections

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| *She likes to swim, run, and **biking**.* | *She likes **to swim**, **to run**, and **to bike**.* / *She likes **swimming**, **running**, and **biking**.* |
| *He is smart, funny, and **has charm**.* | *He is smart, funny, and **charming**.* |
| *I want to travel, learn, and **experiencing** new things.* | *I want **to travel**, **to learn**, and **to experience** new things.* |
| *She is **not only** a singer **but also** she can dance.* | *She is **not only** a singer **but also** a dancer.* / *She **not only** sings **but also** dances.* |
| *He is **either** leaving tomorrow **or** he will stay.* | *He is **either** leaving tomorrow **or** staying.* |
| *The movie was boring, long, and **it was confusing**.* | *The movie was boring, long, and **confusing**.* |

---

### J. Quick Reference Summary

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **In a list of nouns, use all nouns** | *apples, bananas, and oranges* |
| **In a list of adjectives, use all adjectives** | *smart, funny, and kind* |
| **In a list of verbs, use same verb form** | *run, swim, and jump* / *running, swimming, and jumping* |
| **After *both... and...*** | *both smart and kind* |
| **After *either... or...*** | *either stay or leave* |
| **After *neither... nor...*** | *neither rich nor famous* |
| **After *not only... but also...*** | *not only sings but also dances* |
| **In comparisons with *than* or *as*** | *as tall as his brother* / *better than his dancing* |
