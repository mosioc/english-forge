## Appendix C. Vocabulary

Vocabulary is the set of words that a person knows and uses. A strong vocabulary is essential for effective communication in reading, writing, speaking, and listening. This section covers strategies for learning new words, understanding word families, recognizing collocations, and using vocabulary effectively.

> **Related Sections:** See *[Section 1.1 Prefixes and Suffixes](./prefixes-suffixes.md)* for word-building, *[Section 22. Phrasal Verbs](./phrasal-verbs.md)* for multi-word verbs, and *[Section 20.1 Discourse Markers](./discourse-markers.md)* for connecting words.

---

### A. Types of Vocabulary

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Active vocabulary** | Words you can use correctly in speaking and writing | *You use "happy" naturally in sentences.* |
| **Passive vocabulary** | Words you understand but don't actively use | *You understand "ecstatic" but rarely say it.* |
| **General vocabulary** | Common words used in everyday situations | *house, eat, go, good, big* |
| **Academic vocabulary** | Words used in formal and educational contexts | *analyze, significant, therefore, methodology* |
| **Technical vocabulary** | Specialized words for specific fields | *photosynthesis (biology), amortization (finance)* |
| **High-frequency words** | Most common words in the language | *the, be, to, of, and, a, in, that, have, I* |

> **Goal:** Continuously move words from your passive vocabulary to your active vocabulary through practice.

### B. Word Families

A word family is a group of words that share a common base or root. Learning one root can help you understand many related words.

#### 1. Common Root Words

| Root | Meaning | Examples |
|------|---------|----------|
| *scrib/script* | write | *describe, manuscript, subscription, scribble* |
| *dict* | say/speak | *dictate, predict, dictionary, verdict* |
| *port* | carry | *transport, portable, import, porter* |
| *spect* | look | *inspect, spectator, perspective, respect* |
| *aud* | hear | *audio, audience, audible, auditorium* |
| *bio* | life | *biology, biography, antibiotic, biopsy* |
| *chron* | time | *chronic, chronological, synchronize, anachronism* |
| *graph* | write/draw | *autograph, photograph, graphic, paragraph* |
| *log/logue* | word/thought | *dialogue, logic, apology, monologue* |
| *path* | feeling | *sympathy, empathy, pathetic, apathy* |
| *phono* | sound | *telephone, symphony, microphone, phonics* |
| *photo* | light | *photograph, photosynthesis, photogenic* |
| *tele* | far | *television, telephone, telescope, telegraph* |
| *terra* | earth | *territory, terrain, terrestrial, extraterrestrial* |
| *vac* | empty | *vacant, vacuum, vacation, evacuate* |

#### 2. Word Family Example: *Create*

| Part of Speech | Word | Example |
|----------------|------|---------|
| Verb | *create* | *She will **create** a new design.* |
| Noun | *creation, creator, creativity* | *The **creation** took months. He is the **creator**.* |
| Adjective | *creative* | *She has a **creative** mind.* |
| Adverb | *creatively* | *He solved the problem **creatively**.* |
| Negative | *uncreative* | *The solution was **uncreative**.* |

#### 3. Building Word Families with Affixes

| Prefix/Suffix | Meaning | Examples |
|---------------|---------|----------|
| *un-* | not | *happy → unhappy, kind → unkind* |
| *re-* | again | *write → rewrite, build → rebuild* |
| *mis-* | wrong | *understand → misunderstand, lead → mislead* |
| *-able/-ible* | can be done | *read → readable, access → accessible* |
| *-less* | without | *hope → hopeless, care → careless* |
| *-ful* | full of | *hope → hopeful, care → careful* |
| *-tion/-sion* | noun form | *create → creation, decide → decision* |
| *-ness* | noun (state) | *happy → happiness, kind → kindness* |
| *-ly* | adverb form | *quick → quickly, careful → carefully* |

### C. Collocations

Collocations are words that naturally go together. Using correct collocations makes your English sound more natural and fluent.

#### 1. Types of Collocations

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Adjective + Noun** | *strong coffee, heavy rain, fast food, bright future* |
| **Verb + Noun** | *make a decision, do homework, take a break, give advice* |
| **Noun + Verb** | *dogs bark, rain falls, alarm goes off, economy grows* |
| **Adverb + Adjective** | *highly recommended, deeply concerned, extremely important* |
| **Verb + Preposition** | *depend on, belong to, wait for, listen to* |
| **Noun + Preposition** | *reason for, cause of, interest in, solution to* |

#### 2. Common Collocation Errors

| Error (Unnatural) | Correct (Natural) |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| *make a photo* | *take a photo* |
| *do a mistake* | *make a mistake* |
| *say a question* | *ask a question* |
| *tell a lie?* (correct but different) | *tell a lie? (yes, that's fine) / say a lie? (no)* |
| *strong rain* | *heavy rain* |
| *big chance* | *great opportunity* (or *big chance* in informal contexts) |
| *do a decision* | *make a decision* |

#### 3. Common Verb Collocations

| Verb | Common Collocations |
|------|---------------------|
| *make* | *a decision, an effort, a mistake, money, progress, a phone call* |
| *do* | *homework, housework, business, a favor, exercise, your best* |
| *have* | *a problem, fun, a baby, a party, breakfast/lunch/dinner* |
| *take* | *a break, a photo, a shower, a test, advice, responsibility* |
| *give* | *advice, permission, a hand, an example, a speech* |
| *get* | *a job, married, lost, ready, permission, a degree* |
| *keep* | *a secret, calm, quiet, in touch, a promise* |

### E. Homophones, Homonyms, and Homographs

| Term | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Homophones** | Same pronunciation, different spelling/meaning | *there/their/they're, to/too/two, write/right* |
| **Homonyms** | Same spelling, different meaning | *bank (river/money), bat (animal/sports), fair (just/event)* |
| **Homographs** | Same spelling, different pronunciation/meaning | *lead (guide) vs. lead (metal), wind (air) vs. wind (twist)* |

#### Common Homophones

| Pair | Meanings | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| *there/their/they're* | place / possession / they are | ***There** it is. **Their** car. **They're** coming.* |
| *to/too/two* | direction / also / number | *Go **to** school. I'm **too** tired. **Two** people.* |
| *your/you're* | possession / you are | ***Your** book. **You're** late.* |
| *its/it's* | possession / it is | *The dog wagged **its** tail. **It's** raining.* |
| *here/hear* | this place / listen | *Come **here**. I can **hear** you.* |
| *write/right* | to record / correct or direction | *Please **write** a letter. Turn **right**. You are **right**.* |
| *know/no* | to understand / negative | *I **know** the answer. **No**, thank you.* |
| *meet/meat* | encounter / food | *Let's **meet** at noon. I don't eat **meat**.* |
| *see/sea* | perceive / ocean | *I **see** you. Let's go to the **sea**.* |
| *week/weak* | seven days / not strong | *See you next **week**. I feel **weak**.* |

### F. Idioms and Figurative Language

Idioms are expressions whose meaning cannot be understood from the literal meanings of the individual words.

#### 1. Common English Idioms

| Idiom | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| *break the ice* | start a conversation in a social situation | *He told a joke to **break the ice**.* |
| *bite the bullet* | do something difficult or unpleasant | *You just have to **bite the bullet** and apologize.* |
| *hit the nail on the head* | be exactly right | *You **hit the nail on the head** with that analysis.* |
| *cost an arm and a leg* | be very expensive | *That car **costs an arm and a leg**.* |
| *under the weather* | feeling ill | *I'm feeling a bit **under the weather** today.* |
| *piece of cake* | very easy | *The exam was a **piece of cake**.* |
| *spill the beans* | reveal a secret | *Who **spilled the beans** about the party?* |
| *once in a blue moon* | very rarely | *He visits **once in a blue moon**.* |
| *let the cat out of the bag* | reveal a secret accidentally | *I accidentally **let the cat out of the bag**.* |
| *burn the midnight oil* | work late into the night | *She **burned the midnight oil** to finish the project.* |
| *hit the books* | study hard | *I need to **hit the books** for my exam.* |
| *keep an eye on* | watch carefully | *Can you **keep an eye on** my bag?* |
| *call it a day* | stop working for the day | *We're tired. Let's **call it a day**.* |
| *get the hang of* | learn how to do something | *I'm finally **getting the hang of** driving.* |

#### 2. Similes (Comparisons with *like* or *as*)

| Simile | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| *as busy as a bee* | very busy | *She's **as busy as a bee**.* |
| *as light as a feather* | very light | *This box is **as light as a feather**.* |
| *as cold as ice* | very cold | *His hands were **as cold as ice**.* |
| *as brave as a lion* | very brave | *The soldier was **as brave as a lion**.* |
| *like two peas in a pod* | very similar | *They're **like two peas in a pod**.* |
| *sleep like a log* | sleep very soundly | *I **slept like a log** last night.* |

#### 3. Metaphors (Direct Comparisons)

| Metaphor | Meaning | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *time is money* | time is valuable | *Don't waste my time - **time is money**.* |
| *a heart of gold* | very kind person | *She has **a heart of gold**.* |
| *the world is a stage* | life is performance | *He believes **the world is a stage**.* |

### H. Strategies for Learning Vocabulary

#### 1. Effective Techniques

| Strategy | How to Do It |
|----------|--------------|
| **Spaced repetition** | Review words at increasing intervals (1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 1 month) |
| **Context learning** | Learn words in sentences, not in isolation |
| **Word cards (flashcards)** | Write word on one side, definition/example on the other |
| **Word families** | Learn all forms of a word (*create, creation, creative*) |
| **Thematic grouping** | Learn words by topic (e.g., "restaurant vocabulary") |
| **Etymology** | Learn roots, prefixes, and suffixes to guess meanings |
| **Read extensively** | Read books, articles, and news to encounter words naturally |
| **Listen actively** | Listen to podcasts, watch movies/TV with subtitles |
| **Use it or lose it** | Use new words in speaking and writing within 24 hours |

#### 2. Vocabulary Notebook Template

| Word | IPA | Meaning | Example Sentence | Collocations | Word Family |
|------|-----|---------|------------------|--------------|--------------|
| *significant* | /sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt/ | important, notable | *There was a **significant** increase in sales.* | *significant change, significant difference* | *significance (n), significantly (adv)* |

#### 3. The 24-Hour Rule

To move a word from passive to active vocabulary:
- **Learn it** (look up definition, pronunciation, example)
- **Write it** (in a sentence of your own)
- **Say it** (out loud, 3-5 times)
- **Use it** (in conversation or writing within 24 hours)
- **Review it** (after 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 1 month)

### J. Confusing Word Pairs

| Pair | Difference | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| *borrow / lend* | take / give | *Can I **borrow** your pen? Can you **lend** me your pen?* |
| *say / tell* | focus on words / focus on listener | *He **said** hello. He **told** me a story.* |
| *hear / listen to* | perceive sound / pay attention | *I **heard** a noise. I **listened to** the music.* |
| *see / watch / look at* | perceive (without intention) / observe over time / direct attention | *I **saw** a bird. I **watched** the movie. **Look at** this photo.* |
| *raise / rise* | (v) lift something (needs object) / (v) go up (no object) | *She **raised** her hand. The sun **rises**.* |
| *lie / lay* | recline (no object) / put down (needs object) | *I **lie** on the bed. **Lay** the book on the table.* |
| *affect / effect* | (v) influence / (n) result | *The weather **affects** my mood. The **effect** was positive.* |
| *advice / advise* | (n) recommendation / (v) to recommend | *Let me give you some **advice**. I **advise** you to wait.* |
| *practice / practise* (BrE) | (n) / (v) | *Piano **practice** is important. You need to **practise**.* |
| *historic / historical* | important in history / related to history | *a **historic** moment / **historical** documents* |

| Level | Common Error | Correction |
|-------|--------------|------------|

| **Beginner** | *I go to home.* | *I go home.* |

### M. Final Tips for Vocabulary Building

| Tip | Explanation |
|-----|-------------|
| **Learn 5-10 new words per day** | Consistent, small amounts are more effective than cramming |
| **Read at your level +1** | Read material slightly above your current level |
| **Keep a vocabulary journal** | Write new words with definitions and example sentences |
| **Use word maps** | Draw connections between related words |
| **Test yourself regularly** | Use flashcards or apps like Anki, Quizlet |
| **Learn words in chunks** | Learn *make a decision* not just *decision* |
| **Review before sleeping** | Memory consolidates during sleep |
| **Use new words immediately** | Within 24 hours, use the word in speaking or writing |
