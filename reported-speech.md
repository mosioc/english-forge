## 9. Reported Speech (Indirect Speech)

Reported speech is used to convey what someone else said without using their exact words. It involves changes in verb tenses (and modals), pronouns, and time/place expressions.

- **Direct Speech:** *"I am happy," she said.*
- **Reported Speech:** *She said that she was happy.*

> **Note:** The word *that* is optional in reported speech (*She said she was happy* is also correct).

---

### A. Key Changes in Reported Speech

#### 1. Verb Tense Backshift

Generally, verb tenses **move one step back** into the past when reporting.

##### Present -> Past

| Direct Tense | Reported Tense | Example |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Present Simple | Past Simple | *"I **like** pizza," he said.* -> *He said that he **liked** pizza.* |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous | *"I **am studying**," she said.* -> *She said that she **was studying**.* |
| Present Perfect | Past Perfect | *"I **have finished**," he said.* -> *He said that he **had finished**.* |
| Present Perfect Continuous | Past Perfect Continuous | *"I **have been working**," she said.* -> *She said that she **had been working**.* |

##### Past -> Past Perfect

| Direct Tense | Reported Tense | Example |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Past Simple | Past Perfect | *"I **went** home," she said.* -> *She said that she **had gone** home.* |
| Past Continuous | Past Perfect Continuous | *"I **was watching** TV," he said.* -> *He said that he **had been watching** TV.* |

> **Note:** Past Simple can sometimes remain Past Simple if the meaning is clear or the event is very recent.

##### Future -> Conditional

| Direct Tense | Reported Tense | Example |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Future Simple (*will*) | Conditional Simple (*would*) | *"I **will help**," she said.* -> *She said that she **would help**.* |
| Future Continuous (*will be + -ing*) | Conditional Continuous (*would be + -ing*) | *"I **will be waiting**," he said.* -> *He said that he **would be waiting**.* |
| Future Perfect (*will have done*) | Conditional Perfect (*would have done*) | *"I **will have finished** by noon," he said.* -> *He said that he **would have finished** by noon.* |
| *be going to* | *was/were going to* | *"I **am going to call** her," she said.* -> *She said that she **was going to call** her.* |

##### Modal Verbs

| Direct Modal | Reported Modal | Example |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| *can* | *could* | *"I **can swim**," he said.* -> *He said that he **could swim**.* |
| *may* | *might* | *"It **may rain**," she said.* -> *She said that it **might rain**.* |
| *must* | *had to* (or *must*) | *"I **must leave**," he said.* -> *He said that he **had to leave**.* |
| *will* | *would* | *"I **will come**," she said.* -> *She said that she **would come**.* |
| *shall* (suggestion) | *should* | *"**Shall** we go?" he said.* -> *He suggested that they **should go**.* |

> **Modals that do NOT change:** *could, would, should, might, ought to, had better, used to*

---

#### 2. Pronoun Changes

Pronouns change based on the speaker and listener. First and second person usually change to third person.

| Direct Pronoun | Reported Pronoun | Example |
|----------------|------------------|---------|
| *I, me, my* | *he, him, his* / *she, her, hers* | *"**I** am tired," **he** said.* -> *He said that **he** was tired.* |
| *we, us, our* | *they, them, their* | *"**We** need time," **they** said.* -> *They said that **they** needed time.* |
| *you, your* | depends on context (he/she/they/I/we) | *"**Your** car is fast," he said to me.* -> *He told me that **my** car was fast.* |
| *you* (universal/general) | *you* (can remain) | *"**You** should exercise," the doctor said.* -> *The doctor said that **you** should exercise.* |

---

#### 3. Time and Place Expression Changes

Words indicating time and place usually shift away from the moment of speaking.

| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| *now* | *then / at that time / immediately* |
| *today* | *that day* |
| *yesterday* | *the day before / the previous day* |
| *tomorrow* | *the next day / the following day* |
| *last week/month* | *the previous week/month / the week/month before* |
| *next week/month* | *the following week/month* |
| *this* (adjective) | *that* |
| *these* (adjective) | *those* |
| *this* (pronoun) | *it* or *that* |
| *here* | *there* |
| *ago* | *before / earlier* |
| *thus* | *so* |
| *hence* | *thence* (rare) |

**Examples:**
- *"I will see you **tomorrow**," he said.* -> *He said he would see me **the next day**.*
- *"I was **here yesterday**," she said.* -> *She said that she had been **there the day before**.*
- *"I'll finish **this week**," he said.* -> *He said he would finish **that week**.*

---

### B. Reporting Verbs

Common reporting verbs include: *say, tell, ask, inform, explain, reply, answer, exclaim, promise, refuse, offer, suggest, admit, deny, complain, announce, claim, state, mention, confirm, warn, advise, encourage, invite, remind, persuade, convince.*

#### Say vs. Tell (Most Common)

| Verb | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **say** | say (that) / say to someone (that) | *"I'm hungry," he said.* -> *He said (that) he was hungry.* / *He said to me (that) he was hungry.* |
| **tell** | tell someone (that) – always followed by an object | *"I'm hungry," he said to me.* -> *He **told me** (that) he was hungry.* |

> **Note:** *Say* is often followed by *to* when there is an indirect object. *Tell* always requires an object (*tell someone*).

#### Reporting Verbs with Different Patterns

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| *explain* | explain (to someone) + that-clause | *"It's complicated," he said.* -> *He **explained** (to me) that it was complicated.* |
| *admit* | admit + that-clause / admit + -ing | *"I made a mistake," she said.* -> *She **admitted** (that) she had made a mistake.* / *She **admitted making** a mistake.* |
| *deny* | deny + that-clause / deny + -ing | *"I didn't take it," he said.* -> *He **denied** (that) he had taken it.* / *He **denied taking** it.* |
| *suggest* | suggest + that-clause (with *should*) / suggest + -ing | *"Let's go to the cinema," she said.* -> *She **suggested** (that) we (should) go to the cinema.* / *She **suggested going** to the cinema.* |
| *promise* | promise + to-infinitive / promise + that-clause | *"I'll call you," he said.* -> *He **promised to call** me.* / *He **promised** (that) he would call me.* |
| *refuse* | refuse + to-infinitive | *"No, I won't go," she said.* -> *She **refused to go**.* |
| *offer* | offer + to-infinitive | *"I can help you," he said.* -> *He **offered to help** me.* |
| *warn* | warn + someone + to-infinitive / warn + that-clause | *"Don't touch the wire," he said.* -> *He **warned me not to touch** the wire.* / *He **warned** (that) the wire was dangerous.* |
| *advise* | advise + someone + to-infinitive / advise + -ing | *"You should see a doctor," she said.* -> *She **advised me to see** a doctor.* / *She **advised seeing** a doctor.* |
| *encourage* | encourage + someone + to-infinitive | *"Keep trying," he said.* -> *He **encouraged me to keep** trying.* |
| *remind* | remind + someone + to-infinitive / remind + that-clause | *"Don't forget the meeting," she said.* -> *She **reminded me to go** to the meeting.* / *She **reminded me** (that) there was a meeting.* |

---

### C. Reporting Statements: That-Clauses

The most common way to report statements is using a **that-clause** after the reporting verb.

| Direct Statement | Reported Statement |
|-----------------|--------------------|
| *"I love this song," she said.* | *She said **that** she loved that song.* |
| *"The train is late," he announced.* | *He announced **that** the train was late.* |
| *"We have never been to Japan," they told me.* | *They told me **that** they had never been to Japan.* |

> **Note:** *That* can be omitted in informal English: *She said she loved that song.*

---

### D. Reporting Questions

When reporting questions, change the word order back to **normal statement structure** and use a reporting verb like *ask, wonder, inquire, want to know* (no question mark).

#### Yes/No Questions

Use **if** or **whether**.

| Direct Question | Reported Question |
|----------------|-------------------|
| *"Are you tired?" he asked.* | *He asked **if** I was tired.* / *He asked **whether** I was tired.* |
| *"Does she speak English?" they wondered.* | *They wondered **if** she spoke English.* |
| *"Will you be there?" she asked.* | *She asked **whether** I would be there.* |

> **Note:** *Whether* is more formal than *if* and is preferred when choices are explicitly mentioned (*whether... or not*).

#### Wh-Questions

Use the same **wh- word** (*what, where, when, why, how, who, which*).

| Direct Question | Reported Question |
|----------------|-------------------|
| *"Where do you live?" she asked.* | *She asked where I **lived**.* |
| *"What time is it?" he asked.* | *He asked what time it **was**.* |
| *"Why did you leave?" they asked.* | *They asked why I **had left**.* |
| *"How does this work?" she wondered.* | *She wondered how it **worked**.* |
| *"Who called?" he asked.* | *He asked who **had called**.* |

#### Questions with *Who/Which* as Subject (No Inversion)

When the wh-word is the subject of the question, no inversion occurs in direct speech, so the reported speech structure is simpler.

| Direct Question | Reported Question |
|----------------|-------------------|
| *"Who broke the window?" he asked.* | *He asked who **had broken** the window.* |
| *"Which train leaves first?" she asked.* | *She asked which train **left** first.* |

---

### E. Reporting Commands, Requests, and Advice

Use **verb + someone + to-infinitive** for commands, requests, and advice.

| Type | Direct | Reported |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Command** | *"Sit down," the teacher said.* | *The teacher told the students **to sit down**.* |
| **Request** | *"Please help me," she said.* | *She asked me **to help** her.* |
| **Advice** | *"You should see a doctor," he said.* | *He advised me **to see** a doctor.* |
| **Warning** | *"Don't touch that," he said.* | *He warned me **not to touch** that.* |
| **Negative command** | *"Don't be late," she said.* | *She told me **not to be** late.* |
| **Invitation** | *"Would you like to join us?" he said.* | *He invited me **to join** them.* |
| **Reminder** | *"Don't forget to call," she said.* | *She reminded me **to call**.* |

#### Verbs Commonly Used for Commands/Requests

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| *tell* | tell + someone + to-infinitive | *He told me to wait.* |
| *ask* | ask + someone + to-infinitive | *She asked me to help.* |
| *order* | order + someone + to-infinitive | *The general ordered his men to advance.* |
| *command* | command + someone + to-infinitive | *He commanded them to stop.* |
| *beg* | beg + someone + to-infinitive | *She begged him to stay.* |
| *advise* | advise + someone + to-infinitive | *The doctor advised me to rest.* |
| *warn* | warn + someone + to-infinitive | *He warned me not to go.* |
| *remind* | remind + someone + to-infinitive | *She reminded me to call.* |
| *invite* | invite + someone + to-infinitive | *They invited us to join.* |
| *encourage* | encourage + someone + to-infinitive | *He encouraged me to try again.* |

---

### F. Reporting Suggestions, Offers, Promises, Intentions, etc.

Different reporting verbs require different structures.

| Verb | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| *suggest* | suggest + -ing / suggest + that-clause | *"Let's go out," she said.* -> *She **suggested going** out.* / *She **suggested** (that) we **go** out.* |
| *offer* | offer + to-infinitive | *"I can help you," he said.* -> *He **offered to help** me.* |
| *promise* | promise + to-infinitive / promise + that-clause | *"I'll be there," she said.* -> *She **promised to be** there.* / *She **promised** (that) she would be there.* |
| *refuse* | refuse + to-infinitive | *"I won't do it," he said.* -> *He **refused to do** it.* |
| *agree* | agree + to-infinitive | *"Yes, I'll come," she said.* -> *She **agreed to come**.* |
| *threaten* | threaten + to-infinitive | *"I'll call the police," he said.* -> *He **threatened to call** the police.* |
| *propose* | propose + to-infinitive / propose + -ing | *"Let's change the plan," she said.* -> *She **proposed to change** the plan.* / *She **proposed changing** the plan.* |

---

### G. Modal Verbs in Reported Speech

Modal verbs follow specific backshifting rules.

| Direct Modal | Reported Modal | Example |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| *can* | *could* | *"I can swim," he said.* -> *He said he **could** swim.* |
| *may* (possibility) | *might* | *"It may rain," she said.* -> *She said it **might** rain.* |
| *may* (permission) | *could / might* | *"May I leave?" he asked.* -> *He asked if he **could/might** leave.* |
| *must* (obligation) | *had to / must* | *"I must go," she said.* -> *She said she **had to** go.* |
| *must* (deduction) | *must* (no change) | *"He must be tired," she said.* -> *She said he **must** be tired.* |
| *will* | *would* | *"I will call," he said.* -> *He said he **would** call.* |
| *shall* (suggestion) | *should* | *"Shall we dance?" he said.* -> *He suggested that they **should** dance.* |
| *needn't* | *didn't need to / didn't have to* | *"You needn't hurry," she said.* -> *She said I **didn't need to** hurry.* |

> **Modals that do NOT change:** *could, would, should, might, ought to, used to*

---

### H. Reporting What People Say Using Nouns and Adjectives

Reporting can also be done using **nouns** and **adjectives** followed by *that*-clauses or *to*-infinitives.

#### Nouns + That-Clause

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| *noun + that-clause* | *His **claim that** he was innocent was false.* |
| *There is + noun + that-clause* | *There was **a suggestion that** the meeting be postponed.* |
| *make + noun + that-clause* | *She made **the comment that** the food was cold.* |

**Common nouns used in reporting:**
- *claim, statement, comment, remark, suggestion, proposal, demand, request, promise, threat, warning, admission, denial, confession, belief, opinion, view, theory*

#### Adjectives + That-Clause / To-Infinitive

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| *It + be + adjective + that-clause* | *It **is clear that** he made a mistake.* |
| *It + be + adjective + to-infinitive* | *It **is important to note** that sales have increased.* |
| *Subject + be + adjective + to-infinitive* | *She **was happy to report** that the project was complete.* |

**Common adjectives used in reporting:**
- *clear, obvious, evident, apparent, true, false, certain, sure, likely, unlikely, possible, impossible, important, essential, necessary, vital, crucial, interesting, surprising, regrettable, fortunate, unfortunate, helpful, useful*

---

### I. *Should* in That-Clauses and the Present Subjunctive

In formal English, especially British English, **should** can be used in *that*-clauses after certain verbs and adjectives. American English often uses the **present subjunctive** (base form) instead.

| Pattern | Example (British) | Example (American) |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------|
| **suggest + that + should + V1** | *She suggested that he **should go**.* | *She suggested that he **go**.* |
| **recommend + that + should + V1** | *I recommended that she **should apply**.* | *I recommended that she **apply**.* |
| **insist + that + should + V1** | *He insisted that we **should stay**.* | *He insisted that we **stay**.* |
| **demand + that + should + V1** | *They demanded that he **should resign**.* | *They demanded that he **resign**.* |
| **propose + that + should + V1** | *She proposed that the meeting **should be** postponed.* | *She proposed that the meeting **be** postponed.* |
| **it + be + adjective + that + should + V1** | *It is important that he **should arrive** on time.* | *It is important that he **arrive** on time.* |

> **Note:** *Should* is often omitted in American English, leaving the base form of the verb (subjunctive). For more details, see **Section 6.2 Subjunctive Mood**.

---

### J. Reporting Verbs with Wh-Clauses

Some reporting verbs can be followed by a **wh-clause** (what, when, where, why, how, whether).

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| *ask* | *She asked **what time it was**.* |
| *wonder* | *I wondered **why he was late**.* |
| *explain* | *He explained **how the machine worked**.* |
| *describe* | *She described **what she had seen**.* |
| *know* | *I knew **where they had gone**.* |
| *understand* | *I understood **why she was upset**.* |
| *remember* | *He remembered **how they had met**.* |
| *forget* | *She forgot **what she had said**.* |
| *decide* | *They decided **where to go**.* |
| *discuss* | *They discussed **whether to accept the offer**.* |

> **Note:** After these verbs, the wh-clause uses **statement word order** (subject before verb), not question word order.

---

### K. Reporting on the Same Day / No Time Change

When reporting speech on the same day (or when the time reference is still relevant), time and place expressions may **not** need to change.

| Direct | Reported (same day) |
|--------|---------------------|
| *"I'll call you **tomorrow**," he said.* | *He said he will call me **tomorrow**.* (if tomorrow is still tomorrow) |
| *"I saw her **today**," she said.* | *She said she saw her **today**.* (if same day) |
| *"I'm leaving **now**," he said.* | *He said he is leaving **now**.* (if now is still now) |

> **Caution:** If time has passed, the backshift is necessary: *He said he would call me the next day.*

---

### L. Reporting Verbs in Present Tense (No Backshift)

When the reporting verb is in the **present tense**, the verb tense in the reported clause does **not** change. This is common when reporting something that is still true or habitual, or when retelling a story.

| Reporting Verb | Example |
|----------------|---------|
| *says* | *"I like coffee," she **says**.* -> *She **says** she likes coffee.* |
| *tells me* | *"I am tired," he **tells me**.* -> *He **tells me** he is tired.* |
| *explains* | *"The train leaves at 8," she **explains**.* -> *She **explains** that the train leaves at 8.* |

---

### M. Reporting Thoughts (Without Direct Speech)

Reported speech can also be used to report **thoughts**, not just spoken words. Common verbs for thoughts include: *think, believe, feel, consider, realize, understand, suppose, assume, doubt, wonder.*

| Thought (Internal) | Reported Thought |
|--------------------|------------------|
| *"I am lost," he thought.* | *He thought that he **was** lost.* |
| *"She will be late," I thought.* | *I thought that she **would** be late.* |
| *"This is impossible," she realized.* | *She realized that it **was** impossible.* |

---

### N. Exceptions to Backshifting (Complete List)

Tense backshift is **not always necessary**. Here is a complete list of exceptions:

| Exception | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **1. The statement is still true** | *"The Earth **is** round," he said.* -> *He said that the Earth **is** round.* (or *was*) |
| **2. Reporting verb is present tense** | *"I **am** happy," she **says**.* -> *She **says** that she **is** happy.* |
| **3. Reporting a recent conversation** | *"I **have** a new job," John said 5 minutes ago.* -> *John said that he **has** a new job.* (or *had*) |
| **4. General truths / timeless facts** | *"Water **boils** at 100°C," the teacher said.* -> *The teacher said that water **boils** at 100°C.* |
| **5. Habitual actions** | *"I **wake up** at 6 AM every day," he said.* -> *He said that he **wakes up** at 6 AM every day.* |
| **6. Past Simple without time reference** (can remain) | *"I **bought** a car," she said.* -> *She said she **bought** a car.* (if time is irrelevant) |
| **7. Unreal past (wish, would rather, it's time)** | *"I wish I **were** rich," he said.* -> *He said he wished he **were** rich.* (no change) |

### P. Common Errors to Avoid

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *She said me that she was tired.* | *She **told me** that she was tired.* | *Tell* needs an object; *say* does not |
| *He asked where **did I live**.* | *He asked where **I lived**.* | No inversion in reported questions |
| *She asked **that** I was ready.* | *She asked **if** I was ready.* | Use *if/whether* for yes/no questions, not *that* |
| *He told that he was happy.* | *He **said** that he was happy.* | *Tell* requires an object (*told me)* |
| *I asked **what** was the time.* | *I asked **what the time was**.* | Statement word order in reported questions |
| *She suggested **to go** home.* | *She suggested **going** home.* | *Suggest* + gerund or *that*-clause |
| *He promised **that he helps** me.* | *He promised **to help** me.* or *...that he would help me.* | Tense backshift needed |
| *She said that she **would have** called.* (no condition) | *She said that she **would call**.* | *Would have* is for unreal past conditions |
