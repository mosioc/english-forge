## 9. Reported Speech (Indirect Speech)

Reported speech is used to convey what someone else said without using their exact words. It involves changes in verb tenses (and modals), pronouns, and time/place expressions.

- **Direct Speech:** *"I am happy," she said.*
- **Reported Speech:** *She said that she was happy.*

> **Note:** The word *that* is optional in reported speech (*She said she was happy* is also correct).

---

### A. Key Changes in Reported Speech

#### 1. Verb Tense Backshift

Generally, verb tenses **move one step back** into the past when reporting.

##### Present → Past

| Direct Tense | Reported Tense | Example |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Present Simple | Past Simple | *"I **like** pizza," he said.* → *He said that he **liked** pizza.* |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous | *"I **am studying**," she said.* → *She said that she **was studying**.* |
| Present Perfect | Past Perfect | *"I **have finished**," he said.* → *He said that he **had finished**.* |

##### Past → Past Perfect

| Direct Tense | Reported Tense | Example |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Past Simple | Past Perfect | *"I **went** home," she said.* → *She said that she **had gone** home.* |
| Past Continuous | Past Perfect Continuous | *"I **was watching** TV," he said.* → *He said that he **had been watching** TV.* |

> **Note:** Past Simple can sometimes remain Past Simple if the meaning is clear or the event is very recent.

##### Future → Conditional

| Direct Tense | Reported Tense | Example |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Future Simple (*will*) | Conditional Simple (*would*) | *"I **will help**," she said.* → *She said that she **would help**.* |
| Future Continuous (*will be + -ing*) | Conditional Continuous (*would be + -ing*) | *"I **will be waiting**," he said.* → *He said that he **would be waiting**.* |

##### Modal Verbs

| Direct Modal | Reported Modal | Example |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| *can* | *could* | *"I **can swim**," he said.* → *He said that he **could swim**.* |
| *may* | *might* | *"It **may rain**," she said.* → *She said that it **might rain**.* |
| *must* | *had to* (or *must*) | *"I **must leave**," he said.* → *He said that he **had to leave**.* |

> **Modals that do NOT change:** *could, would, should, might, ought to, had better*

---

#### 2. Pronoun Changes

Pronouns change based on the speaker and listener. First and second person usually change to third person.

| Direct Pronoun | Reported Pronoun | Example |
|----------------|------------------|---------|
| *I, me, my* | *he, him, his* / *she, her, hers* | *"**I** am tired," **he** said.* → *He said that **he** was tired.* |
| *we, us, our* | *they, them, their* | *"**We** need time," **they** said.* → *They said that **they** needed time.* |
| *you, your* | depends on context (he/she/they/I/we) | *"**Your** car is fast," he said to me.* → *He told me that **my** car was fast.* |

---

#### 3. Time and Place Expression Changes

Words indicating time and place usually shift away from the moment of speaking.

| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| *now* | *then / at that time* |
| *today* | *that day* |
| *yesterday* | *the day before / the previous day* |
| *tomorrow* | *the next day / the following day* |
| *last week/month* | *the previous week/month / the week/month before* |
| *next week/month* | *the following week/month* |
| *this* | *that* |
| *these* | *those* |
| *here* | *there* |
| *ago* | *before* |

**Examples:**
- *"I will see you **tomorrow**," he said.* → *He said he would see me **the next day**.*
- *"I was **here yesterday**," she said.* → *She said that she had been **there the day before**.*

---

### B. Reporting Verbs

Common reporting verbs include: *say, tell, ask, inform, explain, reply, answer, exclaim, promise, refuse, offer, suggest.*

| Verb | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **say** | say (that) / say to someone (that) | *"I'm hungry," he said.* → *He said (that) he was hungry.* |
| **tell** | tell someone (that) - always followed by an object | *"I'm hungry," he said to me.* → *He **told me** (that) he was hungry.* |
| **ask** | ask someone (question) | See *Reported Questions* below |

> **Note:** *Say* is often followed by *to* when there is an indirect object. *Tell* always requires an object (*tell someone*).

---

### C. Reported Questions

When reporting questions, change the word order back to **normal statement structure** and use a reporting verb like *ask, wonder,* or *inquire* (no question mark).

#### Yes/No Questions

Use **if** or **whether**.

- *"Are you tired?" he asked.*
  - → *He asked **if** I was tired.*
  - → *He asked **whether** I was tired.*

#### Wh-Questions

Use the same **wh- word** (*what, where, when, why, how, who, which*).

- *"Where do you live?" she asked.* → *She asked where I **lived**.*
- *"What time is it?" he asked.* → *He asked what time it **was**.*

---

### D. Reported Commands and Requests

Use **tell someone to do something** for commands and requests.

| Type | Direct | Reported |
|------|--------|----------|
| Affirmative command | *"Sit down," the teacher said.* | *The teacher told the students **to sit down**.* |
| Affirmative request | *"Please help me," she said.* | *She asked me **to help** her.* |
| Negative command | *"Don't touch that," he said.* | *He told me **not to touch** that.* |

> **Other verbs for commands/requests:** *order, command, beg, advise, remind, warn*

---

### E. Exceptions to Backshifting

Tense backshift is **not always necessary**, especially in these situations:

| Exception | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **The statement is still true** | *"The Earth **is** round," he said.* → *He said that the Earth **is** round.* (or *was*) |
| **Reporting verb is present tense** | *"I **am** happy," she **says**.* → *She **says** that she **is** happy.* |
| **Reporting a recent conversation** | *"I **have** a new job," John said 5 minutes ago.* → *John said that he **has** a new job.* (or *had*) |
