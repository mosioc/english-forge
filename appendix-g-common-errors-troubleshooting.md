## Appendix G: Common Errors and Troubleshooting

This section addresses the most frequent mistakes English learners make, organized by category. Use this as a quick reference to identify and correct common errors in your writing and speaking.

> **Related Sections:** See individual topic sections for detailed explanations of each grammar point.

---

### A. Subject-Verb Agreement Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *She **don't** like coffee.* | *She **doesn't** like coffee.* | Third-person singular (he/she/it) takes *does* not *do* |
| *The **students is** waiting.* | *The **students are** waiting.* | Plural subject needs plural verb |
| ***Neither** of the answers **are** correct.* | *Neither of the answers **is** correct.* | *Neither/either* + singular verb (formal English) |
| ***Everyone** have a phone.* | *Everyone **has** a phone.* | Indefinite pronouns (everyone, somebody, nobody) are singular |
| *The **team are** playing well.* (US) | *The **team is** playing well.* (US) | Collective nouns are singular in US English |
| *There **is** many reasons.* | *There **are** many reasons.* | *There* + plural noun → plural verb |
| ***Either** John or his brothers **is** coming.* | *Either John or his brothers **are** coming.* | With *either/or*, verb agrees with the **nearest** subject |
| *A number of students **is** late.* | *A number of students **are** late.* | *A number of* = plural; *The number of* = singular |
| *The news **are** good.* | *The news **is** good.* | *News* is uncountable singular |
| *Each of the students **have** a book.* | *Each of the students **has** a book.* | *Each* + singular verb |
| ***The police is** coming.* | *The police **are** coming.* | *Police* is always plural |
| *Ten dollars **are** enough.* | *Ten dollars **is** enough.* | Amounts of money, time, distance are singular |

### B. Tense and Aspect Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *I **have seen** him yesterday.* | *I **saw** him yesterday.* | Past simple for specific past time |
| *I **am living** here since 2020.* | *I **have lived** here since 2020.* | *Since/for* + present perfect, not present continuous |
| *She **go** to school every day.* | *She **goes** to school every day.* | Third-person singular needs *-s* |
| *When I arrived, she **already left**.* | *When I arrived, she **had already left**.* | Past perfect for action before another past action |
| *I **will call** you when I **will arrive**.* | *I **will call** you when I **arrive**.* | Present tense in future time clauses (*when, if, after*) |
| *He **can** swim when he was five.* | *He **could** swim when he was five.* | Past ability → *could*, not *can* |
| *I **am** here since morning.* | *I **have been** here since morning.* | Action from past to present → present perfect |
| *She **has went** home.* | *She **has gone** home.* | Present perfect needs past participle (*gone*, not *went*) |
| *I **was** never **been** there.* | *I have never **been** there.* | No *was* with *been* in present perfect |
| *By next year, I **finish** my degree.* | *By next year, I **will have finished** my degree.* | Future perfect for completion before a future time |
| *I **have been knowing** her for years.* | *I **have known** her for years.* | Stative verbs (know, believe, understand) not used in continuous |

### C. Modal Verb Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *You **must to** go.* | *You **must** go.* | Modals are followed by base verb, not *to* |
| *She **can** to swim.* | *She **can** swim.* | No *to* after modal verbs |
| *I **don't can** come.* | *I **cannot** (can't) come.* | Negate the modal: *cannot*, not *don't can* |
| *He **must** goes.* | *He **must** go.* | No *-s* on verb after modal |
| *You **should** to see a doctor.* | *You **should** see a doctor.* | No *to* after *should* |
| *I **must** go yesterday.* | *I **had to** go yesterday.* | *Must* has no past form; use *had to* |
| *You **needn't** to go.* | *You **needn't** go.* or *You **don't need to** go.* | *Needn't* + base verb; *don't need* + *to* |
| *He **may** comes.* | *He **may** come.* | No *-s* after modal |
| *You **could** to help me.* | *You **could** help me.* | No *to* after *could* |
| *She **must to be** tired.* | *She **must be** tired.* | No *to* after *must* |
| *I **would** to go if I could.* | *I **would go** if I could.* | No *to* after *would* |

### D. Preposition Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *I depend **of** you.* | *I depend **on** you.* | Specific verb + preposition combinations (memorize) |
| *She is interested **for** art.* | *She is interested **in** art.* | *Interested in* (not *for*) |
| *We arrived **to** the station.* | *We arrived **at** the station.* | *Arrive at* (place) / *arrive in* (city/country) |
| *He is good **in** math.* | *He is good **at** math.* | *Good at* (skill) |
| *I will meet you **in** Monday.* | *I will meet you **on** Monday.* | *On* for days; *in* for months/years |
| *She is afraid **from** spiders.* | *She is afraid **of** spiders.* | *Afraid of* (not *from*) |
| *It depends **about** the weather.* | *It depends **on** the weather.* | *Depends on* |
| *I agree **with** his proposal.* | *I agree **to** his proposal.* or *I agree **with** him.* | *Agree to* (plan/proposal); *agree with* (person) |
| *I'm married **with** John.* | *I'm married **to** John.* | *Married to* (not *with*) |
| *She is good **in** English.* | *She is good **at** English.* | *Good at* a subject |
| *I'm sorry **for** being late.* (acceptable) | *I'm sorry **about** being late.* | Both common, but *about* is standard |
| *We talked **about** the movie.* | Correct | *Talk about* (topic), *talk to/with* (person) |
| *Congratulation **for** your success.* | *Congratulations **on** your success.* | *Congratulations on* (not *for*) |
| *She is typical **from** her family.* | *She is typical **of** her family.* | *Typical of* |

### E. Article (a/an/the) Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *She is **teacher**.* | *She is **a** teacher.* | Singular countable noun needs an article |
| *I have **a** information.* | *I have **some** information.* or *I have information.* | Uncountable nouns cannot take *a/an* |
| ***Sun** is hot.* | ***The** sun is hot.* | Unique nouns (sun, moon, earth) take *the* |
| *I like **the** coffee.* (general) | *I like coffee.* (general) | General statements use no article (or *some*) |
| *He went to **the** hospital.* (US - patient) | *He went to **the** hospital.* (US) / *He went to **hospital**.* (UK) | Regional differences |
| *She is **an** university student.* | *She is **a** university student.* | *A* before consonant sound /juː/ |
| *I need **a** hour.* | *I need **an** hour.* | *An* before vowel sound /aʊər/ |
| *Let's go to **the** bed.* | *Let's go to **bed**.* | No article with *bed, school, prison, church* for purpose |
| *I saw **a** best movie.* | *I saw **the** best movie.* | Superlatives take *the* |
| *French is **the** beautiful language.* | *French is **a** beautiful language.* | Not unique → *a* |
| *She plays **piano**.* | *She plays **the piano**.* | Musical instruments take *the* |
| *I love **the** nature.* | *I love nature.* | Abstract nouns generally take no article |

### F. Pronoun Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *Me and John went.* | *John and I went.* | Subject pronoun (*I*), not object (*me*); put others first |
| *Between you and **I**.* | *Between you and **me**.* | Prepositions take object pronouns (*me, him, her*) |
| *The dog wagged **it's** tail.* | *The dog wagged **its** tail.* | *Its* = possessive (no apostrophe); *It's* = *it is* |
| *Everyone should bring **his** book.* (old) | *Everyone should bring **their** book.* | Singular *they* for gender neutrality |
| *Who did you see? - **Me**.* (informal) | *Who did you see? - **I** did.* (formal) | Formal answer uses subject pronoun |
| *Whom did you see?* (overly formal) | *Who did you see?* | *Who* for object in informal English |
| *The book is **her**.* | *The book is **hers**.* | Possessive pronouns: *mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs* |
| *They love **each other**.* (two) / *They love **one another**.* (three+) | Traditional distinction (often ignored) | ✓ |
| *I hurt **me**.* | *I hurt **myself**.* | Reflexive pronoun needed when subject and object are same |
| *John and **me** are friends.* | *John and **I** are friends.* | Subject position requires *I* |
| *Give it to **they**.* | *Give it to **them**.* | Object position requires object pronoun |

### G. Adjective and Adverb Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *She sings **beautiful**.* | *She sings **beautifully**.* | Use adverb (-ly) to modify verb |
| *I feel **badly**.* (emotion) | *I feel **bad**.* | *Feel* + adjective (linking verb) |
| *This is **more better**.* | *This is **better**.* | Double comparatives are incorrect |
| *She is **more tall** than me.* | *She is **taller** than me.* | One-syllable adjectives take *-er* |
| *He is the **most fastest**.* | *He is the **fastest**.* | Double superlatives are incorrect |
| *I am **more happy**.* | *I am **happier**.* | Two-syllable ending in *-y* → *-ier* |
| *She works **hardly**.* | *She works **hard**.* | *Hard* (diligently) ≠ *hardly* (almost not) |
| *This is a **real** good meal.* | *This is a **really** good meal.* | *Really* modifies adjectives |
| *He is **more taller**.* | *He is **much taller**.* or *He is **taller**.* | Use *much/far/a lot* before comparative |
| *I am **so** happy **that** I could cry.* | Correct (*so... that*) | *So + adjective + that* (result clause) |
| *He ran **quick** to the door.* | *He ran **quickly** to the door.* | Action verb needs adverb |
| *She is **beautifully**.* | *She is **beautiful**.* | After linking verb, use adjective |

### H. Word Order Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *She always **is** late.* | *She **is always** late.* | Adverb of frequency after *be* |
| *I **don't know** what is it.* | *I don't know what it **is**.* | No inversion in embedded questions |
| *He speaks **very well English**.* | *He speaks English **very well**.* | Adverb after object (or after verb with no object) |
| ***Red a car**.* | *A **red car**.* | Adjective before noun (not after article) |
| *I **like very much coffee**.* | *I **like coffee very much**.* | Object before adverb |
| ***What** you want?* (question) | ***What do** you want?* | Questions need auxiliary *do* (except with *be* and modals) |
| *He is **enough tall**.* | *He is **tall enough**.* | *Enough* comes after adjectives/adverbs |
| *I have **very much** money.* | *I have **a lot of** money.* | *Much* is rare in positive statements |
| *She sings **well very**.* | *She sings **very well**.* | *Very* before the adjective/adverb |
| *I saw a man tall.* | *I saw a **tall man**.* | Adjective before noun in English |
| *He gave me a book interesting.* | *He gave me an **interesting book**.* | Adjective before noun |

### I. Double Negative Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *I **don't have no** money.* | *I **don't have any** money.* or *I have **no** money.* | One negative per clause in standard English |
| *She **can't find nothing**.* | *She **can't find anything**.* or *She finds **nothing**.* | Use *any* after negative verb |
| *I **didn't see nobody**.* | *I **didn't see anybody**.* or *I saw **nobody**.* | *Nobody/anybody* - choose one |
| *He **never goes nowhere**.* | *He **never goes anywhere**.* or *He goes **nowhere**.* | *Never* + *anywhere* (not *nowhere*) |
| *I **haven't done nothing**.* | *I **haven't done anything**.* or *I have done **nothing**.* | Choose single negative |
| *She **doesn't know nothing** about it.* | *She **doesn't know anything** about it.* or *She knows **nothing** about it.* | Use single negative |

> **Acceptable double negatives** (for emphasis in some dialects, but avoid in standard English)

### J. Preposition Omission and Addition Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *I discussed **about** the problem.* | *I discussed the problem.* | *Discuss* takes no preposition |
| *She entered **into** the room.* | *She entered the room.* | *Enter* takes no preposition (with place) |
| *We requested **for** help.* | *We requested help.* | *Request* takes no preposition |
| *He lacks **of** confidence.* | *He lacks confidence.* | *Lack* as verb takes no preposition |
| *I called **to** him.* (on phone) | *I called him.* | *Call* someone (no *to*) |
| *Where are you **at**?* (informal) | *Where are you?* | Avoid ending with *at* in formal English |
| *She married **with** him.* | *She married him.* | *Marry* (no preposition) |
| *We reached **to** the destination.* | *We reached the destination.* | *Reach* (no preposition for place) |
| *He resembles **to** his father.* | *He resembles his father.* | *Resemble* (no *to*) |
| *I asked **to** him a question.* | *I asked him a question.* | *Ask* (no preposition) |
| *She mentioned **about** the issue.* | *She mentioned the issue.* | *Mention* takes no preposition |

### K. Common Verb Form Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *I look forward **to see** you.* | *I look forward **to seeing** you.* | *To* in phrasal verb is preposition → + *-ing* |
| *She is used **to work** late.* | *She is used **to working** late.* | *Be used to* + *-ing* (preposition *to*) |
| *I want **that he comes**.* | *I **want him to come**.* | No *that*-clause after *want* |
| *He made me **to laugh**.* | *He made me **laugh**.* | Causative *make* + object + base verb |
| *I let him **to go**.* | *I let him **go**.* | *Let* + object + base verb |
| *I saw him **to leave**.* | *I saw him **leave**.* or *I saw him **leaving**.* | Verbs of perception + base or *-ing* (not *to*) |
| *She suggested **to go**.* | *She suggested **going**.* | *Suggest* + *-ing* (not infinitive) |
| *I enjoy **to read**.* | *I enjoy **reading**.* | *Enjoy* + *-ing* (not infinitive) |
| *He avoids **to see** her.* | *He avoids **seeing** her.* | *Avoid* + *-ing* |
| *I can't help **to laugh**.* | *I can't help **laughing**.* | *Can't help* + *-ing* |
| *I regret **to tell** you that...* (correct - different meaning) | ✓ | *Regret to say* = apologetic; *regret saying* = past regret |
| *She stopped **to smoke**.* (stopped in order to smoke) | *She stopped **smoking**.* (quit) | Meaning changes with infinitive vs. gerund |

### M. Commonly Confused Words

| Pair | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **their / there / they're** | possession / place / they are | ***Their** car is over **there**. **They're** waiting.* |
| **your / you're** | possession / you are | ***Your** bag is here. **You're** late.* |
| **its / it's** | possession / it is | *The dog wagged **its** tail. **It's** raining.* |
| **to / too / two** | direction / also / number 2 | *I'm going **to** school. I'm **too** tired. I have **two** dogs.* |
| **then / than** | time / comparison | *First do this, **then** that. She is taller **than** me.* |
| **lose / loose** | misplace / not tight | *Don't **lose** your keys. The screw is **loose**.* |
| **effect / affect** | (n) result / (v) influence | *The **effect** was positive. It will **affect** the outcome.* |
| **advice / advise** | (n) recommendation / (v) to recommend | *Let me give you some **advice**. I **advise** you to wait.* |
| **accept / except** | receive / exclude | *I **accept** your offer. Everyone came **except** John.* |
| **quiet / quite** | silent / very | *Please be **quiet**. It's **quite** cold.* |
| **weather / whether** | climate / if | *The **weather** is nice. I don't know **whether** to go.* |
| **desert / dessert** | arid land / sweet course | *The Sahara is a **desert**. I love **dessert**.* |
| **principal / principle** | head of school / rule or belief | *The **principal** spoke. It's against my **principles**.* |
| **stationary / stationery** | not moving / writing paper | *The car was **stationary**. I need **stationery**.* |
| **farther / further** | physical distance / metaphorical distance | *I ran **farther** than you. Let's discuss this **further**.* |
| **who's / whose** | who is / possession | ***Who's** coming? ***Whose** bag is this?* |
| **lay / lie** | to put (needs object) / to recline | *She **laid** the book down. I need to **lie** down.* |
| **raise / rise** | to lift (needs object) / to go up (no object) | *She **raised** her hand. The sun **rises**.* |
| **bring / take** | toward speaker / away from speaker | ***Bring** that here. **Take** this to her.* |
| **can / may** | ability / permission | *I **can** swim. **May** I leave now?* |

### N. Punctuation Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *I like coffee she likes tea.* | *I like coffee, **but** she likes tea.* or *I like coffee; she likes tea.* | Comma splice → add conjunction or use semicolon |
| *Its raining.* | *It's raining.* | *It's* = *it is* (apostrophe for contraction) |
| *The dog wagged it's tail.* | *The dog wagged **its** tail.* | No apostrophe for possessive *its* |
| *She said, "I'm tired".* | *She said, "I'm tired."* | Period inside quotation marks (US) |
| *Let's eat Grandma!* | *Let's eat, Grandma!* | Missing comma changes meaning |
| *He is a well known actor.* | *He is a **well-known** actor.* | Hyphen needed in compound adjective before noun |
| *I need eggs milk and bread.* | *I need eggs, milk, and bread.* | Commas in a list (Oxford comma optional) |
| *Whats that?* | *What's that?* | Apostrophe for contraction (*What is*) |
| *She said hello.* (no quotation marks) | *She said, "hello."* | Quotation marks for direct speech |
| *Its a beautiful day.* | *It's a beautiful day.* | Apostrophe for *it is* |
| *The student's are here.* (plural) | *The **students** are here.* | No apostrophe for plural nouns |
| *She has two children's.* | *She has two **children**.* | No apostrophe for plural nouns |

### P. Final Troubleshooting Checklist

Before submitting any written work, check for these common errors:

#### Grammar
- [ ] Subject and verb agree (singular/plural)
- [ ] Verb tense is correct for the time reference
- [ ] No missing *-s* on third-person singular present verbs
- [ ] No double negatives
- [ ] Articles (*a/an/the*) are used correctly with countable/uncountable nouns
- [ ] Prepositions match the verb/adjective (*depend on, interested in*)
- [ ] Pronouns are in correct case (subject/object)
- [ ] Adjectives modify nouns; adverbs modify verbs
- [ ] Modal verbs are followed by base form (no *to*, no *-s*)
- [ ] No missing or extra prepositions (*discuss the problem*, not *discuss about*)
- [ ] Word order is correct (adjective before noun, adverb after object)

#### Punctuation and Spelling
- [ ] No comma splices or run-on sentences
- [ ] Apostrophes are correct (*it's* vs. *its*; plural possessives)
- [ ] Quotation marks are properly placed (periods inside in US English)
- [ ] Hyphens are used in compound adjectives before nouns
- [ ] Commonly confused words are used correctly (*their/there/they're*, *your/you're*)
- [ ] Spelling of common irregular words is correct (*receive*, *separate*, *definitely*)

#### Sentence Structure
- [ ] Each sentence has a subject and a verb
- [ ] No fragments (incomplete sentences)
- [ ] No run-on sentences
- [ ] Parallel structure in lists and comparisons
- [ ] Embedded questions have correct word order (no inversion)
