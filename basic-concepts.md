## 1 Basic Concepts

### I. Sentence Components & Word Types

#### Main Sentence Components

- **Subject:** Who or what performs the action. (e.g., *She* reads.)
- **Verb:** The action or state of being. (e.g., She *reads*.)
- **Object:** Who or what receives the action. (e.g., She reads *a book*.)

#### Types of Verbs

- **Action Verbs:** Express physical or mental action (run, think).
- **Linking Verbs:** Connect subject to a noun or adjective describing it (is, seem, become).
- **Auxiliary Verbs:** Helper verbs (be, do, have) used with main verbs for tenses, voice, etc.
- **All Verbs (Tenses):** Represent time (Present, Past, Future) and aspect (Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous). *See main tense section for details.*
- **Infinitive:** Base form of a verb, often with 'to' (to run, to eat).

#### Nouns

Person, place, thing, or idea (dog, city, happiness).

- **Abstract Nouns:** Concepts or qualities (love, freedom).
- **Concrete Nouns:** Physical things (table, rain).
- **Countable Nouns:** Can be counted (apple, chairs).
- **Uncountable Nouns:** Cannot be counted individually (water, information).
- **Possessive Nouns:** Show ownership using 's or s' (John's book, students' desks).

#### Adjectives

Describe a noun or pronoun (big, happy, red).

- **Comparative:** Compares two things (bigger, happier).
- **Superlative:** Compares three or more (biggest, happiest).
- **Compound Adjectives:** Two or more words acting as one adjective, often hyphenated before a noun (well-behaved child, high-speed train).

#### Adverbs

Modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs (quickly, very, often).

#### Pronouns

Replace a noun (he, she, it, they, who, which).

#### Question Words

Used to ask questions (Who, What, Where, When, Why, How).

#### Prepositions

Show relationship between a noun/pronoun and other words (location, time, direction: in, on, at, under, before).

- **Prepositions of Place:** Indicate location (in, on, at, under, behind).
- **Specific Prepositions:**
  - *With:* Accompaniment, possession (with friends, a cake with frosting).
  - *Over:* Above, more than (over the bridge, over $50).
  - *By:* Nearness, method, agent (by the window, by car, written by her).
- **Verbs After Prepositions:** Usually take the -ing form (gerund) (interested *in learning*, good *at singing*).

#### Articles

Specify nouns (a, an, the).

- *a* / *an*: Indefinite (one of many).
- *the*: Definite (specific one).

#### Conjunctions

Connect words, phrases, or clauses.

- **Coordinating Conjunctions (FANBOYS):** For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So - connect equal elements.
- **Subordinating Conjunctions:** Introduce dependent clauses (because, although, when, if).
- **Adversative Conjunctions:** Show contrast (but, although, however).

#### Prefixes and Suffixes

Word parts added to change meaning or word type.

- **Prefix:** Beginning of a word (un-happy, re-do).
- **Suffix:** End of a word (happi-ness, read-ing).

### II. Sentence Structure & Rules

- **Sentence Rules:** Basic grammar guidelines for forming correct sentences.

- **Rules for Connecting Two Sentences:**
  - Use conjunctions (and, but, so).
  - Use a semicolon (;).
  - Make them separate sentences.

- **Conditional Sentences:** Express conditions and their results (If/When + clause, main clause). Types 0, 1, 2, 3 cover present/future possibilities and past hypotheticals. *See Conditional section for details.*

- **Clauses:** Groups of words with a subject and a verb.
  - **Independent Clause:** Can stand alone as a sentence.
  - **Dependent (Subordinate) Clause:** Cannot stand alone; relies on an independent clause.
    - **Adverbial Clauses:** Act like adverbs, modify verbs/adjectives/adverbs (e.g., *Because it was raining*, we stayed home).
    - **Noun Clauses:** Act like nouns (e.g., *What you said* surprised me).

- **Punctuation:** Marks used to structure writing (., ,, ?, !, :, ;).

- **Active and Passive Voice:**
  - **Active:** Subject performs action (The cat chased the mouse).
  - **Passive:** Subject receives action (The mouse was chased by the cat). *See Passive Voice section for details.*

- **Noun Phrases:** A noun and its modifiers (the big red ball).

- **Adverbial Phrases:** Groups of words acting as an adverb (in the morning, very quickly).

- **Subject-Verb-Object (SVO):** Basic English sentence order.

- **Sentence Structure:** How clauses and phrases are arranged (simple, compound, complex, compound-complex).

### III. Common Auxiliary & Modal Verbs

| Verb / Phrase | Use | Examples |
|---------------|-----|----------|
| **"Going to"** | Future intention or plans | *I am going to study.* |
| **"Could"** | Past ability, possibility, polite request | *I could swim.* / *It could rain.* / *Could you help?* |
| **"Would"** | Past habit, hypothetical, polite request | *We would often go.* / *I would travel.* / *Would you like tea?* |
| **"Do/Does"** | Present simple questions/negatives, emphasis | *Do you like it?* / *She doesn't know.* / *I do agree!* |
| **"Have/Has"** | Present perfect auxiliary, possession | *I have finished.* / *She has a dog.* |
| **"To"** | Part of infinitive, direction, purpose | *to go* / *to the store* / *come to see* |
| **"Used to"** | Past habits/states no longer true | *He used to live here.* |
| **"Wish"** | Regret or desire for change | *I wish I were taller.* |
| **"Will"** | Future certainty, spontaneous decisions, promises | *It will rain.* / *I'll help.* |
| **"Shall"** | Formal future with I/We, offers/suggestions | *I shall arrive.* / *Shall we dance?* |
| **"Have got"** | Possession or obligation (similar to 'have') | *She's got a cat.* / *You've got to go.* |
| **"May/Might"** | Possibility (may = more likely); 'may' also for permission | *It may rain.* / *He might be late.* / *May I leave?* |
