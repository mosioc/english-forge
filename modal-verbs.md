## 6. Modal Verbs

Modal verbs are auxiliary verbs that express modality - such as possibility, ability, necessity, permission, and obligation.

> **Basic Formula:** `Subject + Modal + Base Verb (V1)`  
> *Example:* I (subject) **can** (modal) **swim** (base verb).

**Common modal verbs:** can, could, must, may, might, should, will, would, shall

---

### A. Modals of Possibility, Ability, and Certainty

#### Can

| Use | Meaning | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Ability | Present ability | *I **can** swim.* |
| Possibility | General possibility | *It **can** get very cold here in winter.* |
| Permission (informal) | Ask for or grant permission | *Can I borrow your pen?* / *You **can** leave now.* |

#### Can't

| Use | Meaning | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Impossibility / Certainty (negative) | Something is impossible or very unlikely | *That **can't** be true!* |
| Disbelief | Expressing disbelief | *You **can't** be serious!* |

**Can't Have + Past Participle** - Expresses strong certainty about something that did NOT happen or was impossible.

- *She hasn't arrived yet. She **can't have forgotten** the meeting.* (I am sure she has not forgotten.)
- *He looks exhausted. He **can't have slept** well.* (I deduce he did not sleep well.)

#### Could

| Use | Meaning | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Past ability | Ability in the past | *When I was young, I **could** run very fast.* |
| Possibility | Weaker possibility than *can* | *It **could** rain later, so bring an umbrella.* |
| Permission (polite) | More polite than *can* | *Could I use your phone, please?* |
| Suggestion | Make a suggestion | *We **could** go to the movies tonight.* |

#### (Be) Able To

Similar to *can* and *could*, used for ability. Can be used in all tenses.

| Tense | Form | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| Present | am/is/are able to | *She **is able to** speak three languages.* |
| Past | was/were able to | *He **was able to** finish the project on time.* |
| Future | will be able to | *They **will be able to** join us tomorrow.* |

#### Must

| Use | Meaning | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Certainty / Strong belief | Strong belief that something is true | *He **must** be tired after that long journey.* |
| Necessity / Obligation (strong) | Strong sense of obligation | *You **must** wear a helmet when cycling.* |

#### May

| Use | Meaning | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Possibility | Expresses a possibility | *It **may** snow tomorrow.* |
| Permission (formal) | Ask for or grant permission formally | *May I come in?* / *You **may** begin the exam now.* |

**May As Well + Base Verb** - Suggests there is no better alternative; carries a sense of resignation or practicality.

- *It's raining, and we have no plans. We **may as well stay** in and watch a movie.*
- *If you're going to be late, you **may as well call** and let me know.*

#### Might

| Use | Meaning | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Possibility | Weaker possibility than *may* | *He **might** call later, but don't count on it.* |
| Suggestion | Similar to *could* | *You **might** want to check the expiry date.* |

---

### B. Modals of Obligation and Necessity

| Modal | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **must** | Strong obligation (speaker's perspective) | *I **must** finish this report by Friday.* |
| **have to** | External obligation (rules, external factors) | *Students **have to** wear uniforms.* / *You **have to** have a passport to travel.* |
| **needn't** | Lack of necessity | *You **needn't** worry about the test; it's quite easy.* |

#### Needn't Have vs. Didn't Need To

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **needn't have + V3** | Action was unnecessary but was done anyway | *We **needn't have bought** so much food.* (We bought it, but it wasn't necessary.) |
| **didn't need to + V1** | Action was unnecessary and did NOT happen | *We had plenty of time, so we **didn't need to hurry**.* (We didn't hurry.) |

#### Wasn't / Weren't Able To (Past Inability)

- *I **wasn't able to** solve the puzzle.*
- *They **weren't able to** find a solution.*

---

### C. Modals of Advice, Recommendation, and Strong Suggestion

| Modal | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **should** | Advice / recommendation; expectation | *You **should** see a doctor.* / *He **should** be home by now.* |
| **ought to** | Similar to *should* (sometimes stronger moral obligation) | *We **ought to** help those in need.* |
| **had better** | Strong advice / warning (with negative consequences) | *You **had better** apologize, or she'll never speak to you again.* |
| **it's time / high time** | Urgency - something should be done now | *It's time we left for the airport.* / *It's high time you started studying.* |

---

### D. Modals in Past Forms (Hypothetical Situations)

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **could have + V3** | Unrealized past ability/possibility (didn't happen) | *I **could have gone** to the party, but I stayed home.* |
| **should have + V3** | Regret / criticism (didn't happen, or shouldn't have happened) | *I **should have studied** harder.* / *You **shouldn't have said** that.* |
| **must have + V3** | Strong belief about the past | *She **must have received** good news.* |
| **might have + V3** | Past possibility (may or may not have happened) | *He **might have forgotten** our meeting.* |
| **may have + V3** | Past possibility (similar to *might have*) | *The keys **may have fallen** out of my pocket.* |

---

### E. Degree of Certainty (Present and Past)

| Certainty Level | Present Form | Past Form |
|----------------|--------------|-----------|
| **High (Likely)** | *must + V1* | *must have + V3* |
| Example | *He **must be** very tired.* | *She **must have arrived** by now.* |
| **Medium (Possible)** | *may / might / could + V1* | *may / might / could have + V3* |
| Example | *He **may be** stuck in traffic.* | *They **might have missed** the bus.* |
| **Low / Negative (Impossible)** | *can't / couldn't + V1* | *can't / couldn't have + V3* |
| Example | *That **can't be** true!* | *He **can't have forgotten** the time.* |

---

### F. Semi-Modals

Semi-modals are verb phrases that function like modal verbs but are not single words. They often behave more like regular verbs (taking `-ing`, `-ed`, etc.) but express modal meanings.

| Semi-Modal | Function | Example |
|------------|----------|---------|
| **have to** | Obligation/necessity (external) | *I **have to** finish this report.* |
| **ought to** | Advice / moral obligation | *You **ought to** apologize.* |
| **(be) supposed to** | Expectation or plan | *The meeting **is supposed to** start at 10.* |
| **(be) able to** | Ability (all tenses) | *I **will be able to** help you.* |
| **tend to** | Habitual action or general tendency | *She **tends to** be late.* |
| **be likely to** | Probability | *It **is likely to** rain later.* |

---

### G. Quick Reference: Modal Verb Summary

| Modal | Primary Uses |
|-------|--------------|
| **can** | ability, possibility, informal permission |
| **could** | past ability, polite permission, possibility, suggestion |
| **must** | strong obligation, strong certainty |
| **may** | formal permission, possibility |
| **might** | weak possibility, suggestion |
| **should** | advice, recommendation, expectation |
| **ought to** | advice (moral obligation) |
| **had better** | strong advice (with warning) |
| **have to** | external obligation |
| **needn't** | lack of necessity |
