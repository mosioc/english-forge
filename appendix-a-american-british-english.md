## Appendix A. American vs. British English

English grammar is mostly the same in the UK and the US, but there are systematic differences in tense usage, verb patterns, modal verbs, vocabulary choices, and spelling. Below is a clear guide to the most important distinctions.

---

### A. Tense Usage

#### 1. Present Perfect vs. Past Simple

| Context | British English | American English |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| Recent events / new information | Present perfect: *I've lost my key. Have you seen it?* / *Sally isn't here. She's gone out.* | Past simple: *I lost my key. Did you see it?* / *Sally isn't here. She went out.* |
| With *just, already, yet* | *I've just had lunch.* / *He's already left.* | *I just had lunch.* / *He already left.* |

---

### B. Verb Patterns and Choices

#### 2. Common Verbs: *Have* vs. *Take*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *have a bath / have a shower* | *take a bath / take a shower* |
| *have a break / have a holiday* | *take a break / take a vacation* |

#### 3. *Shall* vs. *Will* / *Should*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *I will/shall be late this evening.* | *I will be late this evening.* (*Shall* is rare) |
| *Shall I...? / Shall we...?* (suggestions) | *Should I...? / Should we...?* (replaces *Shall*) |

#### 4. Probability: *Can't* vs. *Must Not*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *She **can't** have got my message.* | *She **must not** have gotten my message.* |

#### 5. *Needn't* vs. *Don't Need To*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *We **needn't** hurry.* / *We **don't need to** hurry.* (both common) | *We **don't need to** hurry.* (*needn't* is very uncommon) |

#### 6. Verbs after *Insist / Demand / Suggest*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Often use *should*: *I insisted that he **should apologise**.* / *We demanded that something **should be done**.* | Use the **subjunctive**: *I insisted that he **apologize**.* / *We demanded that something **be done**.* |

#### 7. Question Tags

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *Lisa isn't very well today.* - *Isn't she? What's wrong?* | Question tags are less frequent; repetition is common: *Lisa isn't very well today.* - *She isn't? What's wrong?* |

#### 8. *I'd Rather* + Past vs. Present

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *I'd rather you **did** something.* / *Would you rather I **told** her?* | *I'd rather you **do** something.* / *Would you rather I **tell** her?* |

---

### C. Nouns and Articles

#### 9. *Accommodation*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Uncountable: *There is plenty of excellent **accommodation**.* | Can be countable: *There are plenty of excellent **accommodations**.* |

#### 10. *Hospital*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Without *the*: *He was taken to **hospital**.* | With *the*: *He was taken to **the hospital**.* |

#### 11. Collective Nouns (e.g., *team, government, company*)

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Singular or plural verb: *The team **is/are** playing well.* | Generally singular: *The team **is** playing well.* |

---

### D. Prepositions

#### 12. *Weekend*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *at the weekend / at weekends* | *on the weekend / on weekends* |

#### 13. Position: *Front / Back*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *Let's sit **at** the front.* | *Let's sit **in** the front.* |

#### 14. *Different From / To / Than*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *different **from*** or *different **to*** | *different **from*** or *different **than*** |

#### 15. *Round / Around*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *He turned **round**.* or *He turned **around**.* | *He turned **around**.* (only) |

---

### E. Phrasal Verbs

| Meaning | British English | American English |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|
| Complete a form | *fill **in** / fill **out*** | *fill **out*** (only) |
| Have a good relationship | *get **on** (with someone)* | *get **along** (with someone)* |
| Demolish buildings | *The houses were **knocked down**.* | *The houses were **torn down**.* |
| Renovate | *They've **done up** the house.* | *They've **fixed up** the house.* |

---

### F. Spelling Differences

#### 16. *-ise* vs. *-ize* (verbs)

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *apolog**ise**, organ**ise**, special**ise*** | *apolog**ize**, organ**ize**, special**ize*** |

> **Note:** Both forms are acceptable in the UK, but *-ise* is more common.

#### 17. Regular vs. Irregular Past Forms

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *burned / **burnt**, spelled / **spelt*** (both common) | ***burned**, **spelled*** (regular forms preferred) |

#### 18. Past Participle of *Get*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| ***got***: *Your English has **got** much better.* | ***gotten***: *Your English has **gotten** much better.* |

> **Note:** *Have got* (= *have*) is the same in both: *I've got a car.*

#### 19. Doubling Consonants before *-ing* / *-ed*

| British English | American English |
|-----------------|------------------|
| *trav**elling**, trav**elled** / canc**elling**, canc**elled*** | *trav**eling**, trav**eled** / canc**eling**, canc**eled*** |

---

### G. Quick Reference Summary Table

| Category | British English | American English |
|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Recent past** | Present perfect (*I've lost...*) | Past simple (*I lost...*) |
| **Bath/Shower** | *have a bath* | *take a bath* |
| **Suggestion** | *Shall I...?* | *Should I...?* |
| **Impossibility** | *can't have* | *must not have* |
| **Lack of necessity** | *needn't* (common) | *don't need to* (only) |
| **Subjunctive** | *should* + verb | base verb (*that he be*) |
| **Question tags** | Frequent | Less frequent |
| **Hospital** | *to hospital* | *to the hospital* |
| **Weekend** | *at the weekend* | *on the weekend* |
| **Get (past participle)** | *got* | *gotten* |
| **Spelling (-ise/-ize)** | *-ise* (common) | *-ize* (only) |
| **Doubling (travel)** | *travelling* | *traveling* |
