## 8. Passive Voice

The passive voice is used when the focus is on the action itself, not on who or what performs it. The object of an active sentence becomes the subject of a passive sentence, and the doer (agent) is often omitted when unknown, unimportant, or obvious.

- **Active:** Mehdi broke the window.
- **Passive:** The window was broken (by Mehdi).

> All passive tenses end in the past participle (V3).

### A. Formation Basics

The passive voice is formed using the appropriate tense of **be** + past participle (V3).

**Conjugation of "to be":**
- Present: am/is/are
- Past: was/were
- Future: be

**Conjugation of "to have" (for perfect tenses):**
- Present: has (he, she, it) / have (I, you, we, they)
- Past: had (all subjects)
- Future: will have (all subjects)

### B. Simple Tenses (be + V3)

| Tense | Form | Examples |
|-------|------|----------|
| Infinitive Passive | be + V3 | *The work needs **to be done**.* |
| Present Simple | am/is/are + V3 | *English **is spoken** in many countries.* / *Mistakes **are made**.* / *The door **is opened** by the cleaner every morning.* |
| Past Simple | was/were + V3 | *The window **was broken** yesterday.* / *They **were invited** to the party.* / *The book **was written** last year.* |
| Future Simple | will + be + V3 | *The package **will be delivered** tomorrow.* / *The report **will be finished** by Friday.* |

### C. Continuous Tenses (be + being + V3)

| Tense | Form | Examples |
|-------|------|----------|
| Present Continuous | am/is/are + being + V3 | *The house **is being built**.* / *The food **is being prepared**.* / *His actions **are being watched**.* |
| Past Continuous | was/were + being + V3 | *The bridge **was being repaired** when the storm hit.* / *They **were being interviewed** at that time.* |

### D. Perfect Tenses (have/has/had + been + V3)

| Tense | Form | Examples |
|-------|------|----------|
| Present Perfect | have/has + been + V3 | *The email **has been sent**.* / *The project **has been completed**.* / *Many mistakes **have been made**.* |
| Past Perfect | had + been + V3 | *The problem **had been solved** before we arrived.* / *They realized the car **had been stolen**.* |

### E. Modal Verbs in Passive (modal + be + V3)

*Form:* modal verb + be + past participle

- *This car **can be rented** by the hour.*
- *The rules **must be followed**.*
- *You **should be careful**.*
- *The work **might be finished** soon.*
- *That **could be seen** from a distance.*

### F. Personal vs. Impersonal Passive

**Personal Passive** - The direct object of an active sentence becomes the subject.

- Active: *The chef prepared the meal.*
- Personal Passive: *The meal was prepared by the chef.* (past simple passive: be + (v3))
- Active: *The committee will announce the results.*
- Personal Passive: *The results will be announced by the committee.* (future simple passive: will + be + (v3))

**Impersonal Passive** - Used with verbs that do not take a direct object or when generalizing opinions/beliefs.

- **Using "it" as a dummy subject** (with verbs of saying, believing, thinking, reporting):
  - Active: *People say that honesty is the best policy.* → *It is said that honesty is the best policy.*
  - Active: *They believe that the project will succeed.* → *It is believed that the project will succeed.*
  - Active: *Authorities report that the situation is under control.* → *It is reported that the situation is under control.*

- **Using the indirect object as the subject** (with verbs like *tell, ask, give, show, teach*):
  - Active: *Someone told me that the meeting was cancelled.* → *I was told that the meeting was cancelled.*
  - Active: *The teacher gave the students a difficult assignment.* → *The students were given a difficult assignment.*

> **Two passive forms from one active sentence:** When an active sentence has both a direct and an indirect object, two different passive sentences are possible.
> - Active: *The company offered him a new position.*
>   - Personal Passive (direct object as subject): *A new position was offered to him.*
>   - Impersonal Passive (indirect object as subject): *He was offered a new position.*

### G. Reporting Verbs in Passive

- **It is said that...** / **He is said to...**
  - Active: *People say that he is a genius.*
  - Passive: *It is said that he is a genius.* / *He is said to be a genius.*

- **He is supposed to...** (expectation or obligation)
  - Active: *They expect him to be on time.*
  - Passive: *He is supposed to be on time.* / *They are supposed to finish the report today.*

### H. Have Something Done (Causative)

*Form:* have/had + object + past participle (V3)

Used when someone arranges for something to be done by another person.

- *I had my hair cut yesterday.* (Someone else cut my hair.)
- *She is having her car repaired.* (A mechanic is repairing her car.)
- *He had his phone stolen.* (Someone stole his phone - unfortunate event.)

### I. The Agent (by Phrase)

The *by* phrase identifies the performer of the action. Include it when the agent is known and important; omit it when unknown, unimportant, or obvious.

- **Agent known and important:** *The Mona Lisa was painted by Leonardo da Vinci.* / *The email was sent by Sarah.*
- **Agent unknown:** *My bike was stolen.*
- **Agent unimportant/obvious:** *The road is being repaired.* / *The meeting will be held in the conference room.*

### J. Passive in Conditional Statements

- **First Conditional (if + present simple, will + be + V3):**
  - Active: *If the company hires more staff, productivity will increase.*
  - Passive: *If more staff are hired, productivity will increase.*

- **Second Conditional (if + past simple, would + be + V3):**
  - Active: *If I had more time, I would learn a new language.*
  - Passive: *If more time were given to me, I would learn a new language.*

- **Third Conditional (if + past perfect, would have + been + V3):**
  - Active: *If the team had won the match, they would have advanced.*
  - Passive: *If the match had been won, they would have advanced.*

### K. Passive in Imperative Statements

*Form:* Let + object + be + past participle (formal instructions)

- *Let the door be locked after everyone has left.*
- *Let the engine be checked before the next flight.*

**Alternative (strong commands with modals):**
- *The report must be submitted by Friday.*
- *All guests must be registered at the reception.*

### L. Active vs. Passive: Choosing the Right Voice

**Prefer Active Voice when:**
- You want directness and clarity: *The team completed the project ahead of schedule.*
- You want stronger impact: *The company launched a new product.*
- You need to assign responsibility: *John made this mistake.*
- You want to avoid wordiness.

**Prefer Passive Voice when:**
- The receiver of the action is more important: *The archaeological site was discovered in 1922.*
- The agent is unknown, unimportant, or obvious: *My wallet has been stolen.* / *The results will be announced tomorrow.*
- You need objectivity or formality (scientific, academic, official writing): *The samples were analyzed using a mass spectrometer.* / *It was decided that the meeting should be postponed.*
- You want to vary sentence structure or avoid assigning blame: *Mistakes were made.*

> **Key Strategy:** Ask yourself three questions:
> 1. Who or what is the most important element here? (Receiver → passive; Doer → active)
> 2. Do I need to name the performer of the action? (Yes → usually active)
> 3. What tone do I want to convey? (Directness → active; Objectivity/formality → passive)
