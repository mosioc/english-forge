## 2. Regular and Irregular Verbs

All verbs in English have three main forms: the **infinitive (base form / V1)**, the **past simple (V2)**, and the **past participle (V3)**.

- **Infinitive (V1) examples:** clean, finish, use, paint, stop, carry
- **Past simple (V2) and past participle (V3) examples:** cleaned, finished, used, painted, stopped, carried

---

### A. Regular Verbs

If a verb is regular, the past simple and past participle end in **-ed**.

> **Rule:** Add `-ed` to the base form (V1).  
> *Examples: I cleaned / they finished / she carried, etc.*

#### Past Simple (V2) vs. Past Participle (V3)

| Form | Use | Needs Auxiliary? | Example |
|------|-----|------------------|---------|
| **Past Simple (V2)** | Describes a completed action in the past on its own | **No** - used independently | *I cleaned my room.* (The action of cleaning is completed in the past.)<br>*She finished her work.* |
| **Past Participle (V3)** | Used in perfect tenses and passive voice | **Yes** - always needs an auxiliary verb (`have`, `has`, `had`, or a form of `be`) | *I have cleaned my room.* (Present perfect)<br>*She had finished her work before the deadline.* (Past perfect)<br>*My room was cleaned by my brother.* (Passive voice)<br>*The work has been finished.* (Passive, present perfect) |

> **Summary:** We use the past participle (V3) to form all perfect tenses (`have/has/had + V3`) and all passive forms (`be + V3`).

---

### B. Irregular Verbs

When the past simple and past participle do **not** end in `-ed` (e.g., *I saw / I have seen*), the verb is irregular.

Irregular verbs fall into three main patterns:

#### Pattern 1: All three forms are the same

| Infinitive (V1) | Past Simple (V2) | Past Participle (V3) |
|-----------------|------------------|----------------------|
| hit | hit | hit |
| cut | cut | cut |
| put | put | put |
| set | set | set |

#### Pattern 2: Past simple = Past participle (different from infinitive)

| Infinitive (V1) | Past Simple (V2) | Past Participle (V3) |
|-----------------|------------------|----------------------|
| tell | told | told |
| bring | brought | brought |
| keep | kept | kept |
| feel | felt | felt |

#### Pattern 3: All three forms are different

| Infinitive (V1) | Past Simple (V2) | Past Participle (V3) |
|-----------------|------------------|----------------------|
| wake | woke | woken |
| speak | spoke | spoken |
| sing | sang | sung |
| go | went | gone |

---

### C. Verbs That Can Be Regular or Irregular

The following verbs have both regular (`-ed`) and irregular forms. Both are generally understood.

| Verb | Regular Form | Irregular Form |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| burn | burned | burnt |
| dream | dreamed | dreamt |
| lean | leaned | leant |
| learn | learned | learnt |
| smell | smelled | smelt |
| spell | spelled | spelt |
| spill | spilled | spilt |
| spoil | spoiled | spoilt |

> **Note:** The irregular form is often more common in **British English**, while the regular `-ed` form is often more common in **American English**. However, both are accepted and understood in all varieties of English.
