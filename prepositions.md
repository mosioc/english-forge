## 21. Prepositions

Prepositions are words that typically precede a noun or pronoun (the object of the preposition) to show its relationship with another word in the sentence. This relationship can indicate location, time, direction, manner, cause, possession, or purpose.

> **Basic Pattern:** *Preposition + Noun / Pronoun / Gerund (-ing)*

> **Related Sections:** See *[Section 20. Conjunctions](./conjunctions.md)* for conjunctions and the conjunction vs. preposition distinction.

---

### A. Types of Relationships Shown by Prepositions

| Relationship | Function | Examples |
|--------------|----------|----------|
| **Place/Location** | Where something is | *The book is **on** the table.* / *She lives **in** London.* / *He is waiting **at** the bus stop.* |
| **Time** | When something happens | *The meeting is **at** 3 PM.* / *My birthday is **in** July.* / *I will see you **on** Friday.* |
| **Direction/Movement** | Where something is going | *He walked **across** the street.* / *She dived **into** the water.* / *The bird flew **over** the house.* |
| **Manner** | How something is done | *He spoke **with** great enthusiasm.* / *She arrived **by** train.* |
| **Cause/Reason** | Why something happened | *He was tired **from** walking.* / *They suffered **from** lack of sleep.* / *The delay was **due to** bad weather.* |
| **Purpose** | What something is used for | *This is a tool **for** cutting.* / *She bought a gift **for** her mother.* |
| **Possession/Association** | Ownership or connection | *The cover **of** the book.* / *A friend **of** mine.* / *He has a great sense **of** humor.* |
| **Agent (Passive Voice)** | Who performs the action | *The ball was thrown **by** the pitcher.* / *The letter was written **by** her.* |
| **Comparison/Quantity** | Comparing or indicating amount | *It costs **over** $50.* / *He is taller **than** his brother.* / ***About** ten students are absent.* |

### B. Common Prepositions and Their Uses

#### 1. Prepositions of Time

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| *at* | Specific clock times, mealtimes, holidays (without 'day') | *at 5:00 PM, at noon, at midnight, at dinnertime, at Christmas* / Phrases: *at the moment, at night* |
| *on* | Specific days and dates | *on Monday, on December 25th, on my birthday, on New Year's Day* / *on the weekend* (US) |
| *in* | Longer periods: months, seasons, years, decades, centuries, parts of the day (except night) | *in January, in summer, in 2024, in the 1990s, in the morning/afternoon/evening* |
| *since* | Starting point in the past (with present/past perfect) | *I have lived here **since** 2015.* / *She hasn't eaten meat **since** last Tuesday.* |
| *for* | Duration (period of time) | *We waited **for** two hours.* / *He has been sick **for** a week.* |
| *during* | Within a specific period | *Don't talk **during** the movie.* / *Changes occurred **during** the Renaissance.* |
| *before* | Earlier than a specific time/event | *Call me **before** 8 PM.* / *Finish your homework **before** you play.* |
| *after* | Later than a specific time/event | *We will meet **after** the conference.* / *He felt better **after** taking the medicine.* |
| *until / till* | Duration up to a specific point | *Please wait here **until** I return.* / *They talked **till** midnight.* |
| *by* | Not later than (deadline) | *Please submit the report **by** Friday.* |
| *by the time* | At or before a specific moment in the future | *By the time you arrive, dinner will be ready.* |
| *through* | Throughout a period (including beginning to end) | *The store is open **through** the holidays.* |

**Idiomatic Phrases for Time:**

| Phrase | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| *on time* | Exactly at the scheduled time; punctual | *The train arrived **on time**.* |
| *in time* | With enough time to spare before a deadline | *We got to the airport **in time** to catch our flight.* |
| *at the end (of)* | The final part of a period | ***At the end of** the day, we were all happy.* |
| *in the end* | Finally, after a long process or difficulty | ***In the end**, they decided to sell the house.* |

> **Note on *during* vs. *while*:** *During* is a **preposition** and must be followed by a noun or noun phrase. *While* is a **conjunction** and must be followed by a clause (subject + verb). See **[Section 20. Conjunctions](./conjunctions.md)** and the preposition vs. conjunction distinction in **[Section 21.F](#f-prepositions-vs-conjunctions-key-distinction)**.
>
> | Preposition (*during* + noun) | Conjunction (*while* + clause) |
> |------------------------------|-------------------------------|
> | *Don't talk **during** the movie.* | *Don't talk **while** the movie is playing.* |
> | *She worked **during** the night.* | *She worked **while** the children slept.* |
> | *He fell asleep **during** the meeting.* | *He fell asleep **while** the boss was speaking.* |

#### 2. Prepositions of Place / Position

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| *in* | Enclosed spaces; larger areas (cities, countries, rooms) | *in a box, in a room, in Paris, in the ocean* / Phrases: *in the picture, in the newspaper* |
| *on* | Surfaces; specific locations on a line (streets, rivers) | *on the table, on the wall, on Baker Street, on the river* / *on the floor, on the page, on TV* |
| *at* | Specific points or locations | *at the door, at the corner, at the bus stop, at the airport, at school, at home* |
| *to* | Direction towards a destination | *He went **to** the store.* / *She travels **to** Japan often.* |
| *into* | Movement from outside to inside | *She walked **into** the house.* / *The car crashed **into** the wall.* |
| *onto* | Movement to a surface | *He jumped **onto** the table.* |
| *over* | Movement above something; covering something | *The plane flew **over** the city.* / *Put the blanket **over** him.* |
| *under* | Below something | *The cat is hiding **under** the bed.* / *The temperature dropped **under** zero.* |
| *above* | Higher than (without touching) | *The light hangs **above** the table.* |
| *below* | Lower than (without touching) | *The village lies **below** the mountain.* |
| *between* | Two people, things, or points | *The house is **between** the trees.* / *Divide the pizza **between** the two friends.* |
| *among* | Three or more people, things, or points | *He felt lost **among** the crowd.* / *Share the candies **among** the children.* |
| *through* | Movement from one side to the other; passing within something | *They walked **through** the forest.* / *The light shone **through** the window.* |
| *across* | Movement from one side to the other (of a surface) | *He swam **across** the lake.* / *There is a shop **across** the street.* |
| *along* | Following the length of something | *We walked **along** the beach.* |
| *against* | In contact with (often opposition) | *The ladder is leaning **against** the wall.* |
| *near* | Close to | *She lives **near** the station.* |
| *beside* | Next to | *She sat **beside** me during the flight.* |
| *behind* | At the back of | *The garden is **behind** the house.* |
| *in front of* | Before / opposite | *The car parked **in front of** the building.* |
| *opposite* | Across from | *The bank is **opposite** the post office.* |

#### 3. Prepositions Following Specific Word Types

Many verbs, adjectives, and nouns are followed by specific prepositions. This is often idiomatic and needs to be learned.

##### Verb + Preposition

| Preposition | Verbs |
|-------------|-------|
| *to* | *listen to, speak to, talk to, give to, send to, travel to, belong to, refer to, respond to* |
| *at* | *look at, laugh at, aim at, arrive at* (specific point/event), *stare at, shout at* |
| *about* | *talk about, think about, worry about, ask about, argue about, dream about* |
| *for* | *wait for, look for, ask for, hope for, search for, apply for, care for, pay for* |
| *of* | *hear of, dream of, think of, consist of, approve of, be proud of, die of* |
| *from* | *hear from, come from, suffer from, protect from, recover from, differ from* |
| *on* | *depend on, work on, comment on, focus on, rely on, insist on, spend on* |
| *in* | *believe in, succeed in, result in, participate in, engage in, invest in* |
| *into* | *break into, get into, turn into, look into (investigate)* |
| *with* | *agree with (a person), deal with, provide with, be satisfied with, compare with* |
| *across* | *come across (find by chance), run across* |

##### Adjective + Preposition

| Preposition | Adjectives |
|-------------|------------|
| *of* | *afraid of, proud of, sure of, aware of, tired of, fond of, jealous of, capable of, full of* |
| *for* | *sorry for, responsible for, famous for, good for, ready for, known for, late for* |
| *from* | *different from, far from, safe from, absent from, recovered from, protected from* |
| *on* | *keen on, dependent on, based on, focused on* |
| *in* | *interested in, involved in, rich in, successful in* |
| *at* | *good at, bad at, surprised at, excellent at, amazed at, skilled at* |
| *with* | *pleased with, familiar with, compatible with, satisfied with, crowded with* |
| *about* | *worried about, curious about, excited about, happy about, nervous about* |
| *to* | *similar to, married to, engaged to, related to, loyal to, accustomed to* |

##### Noun + Preposition

| Preposition | Nouns | Examples |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| *for* | *demand for, need for, reason for, taste for, respect for, love for* | *There is a high **demand for** skilled workers.* / *She has a **taste for** adventure.* |
| *of* | *lack of, cause of, result of, fear of, sense of, way of, method of* | *The **lack of** rain caused a drought.* / *He has a **sense of** humor.* |
| *to* | *solution to, answer to, reaction to, damage to, invitation to, key to* | *We need a **solution to** the problem.* / *The accident caused **damage to** the car.* |
| *with / between* | *relationship with, relationship between, connection with, contact with* | *Her **relationship with** her boss is good.* / *The **connection between** the two events is unclear.* |
| *in* | *increase in, decrease in, interest in, faith in, confidence in* | *There was an **increase in** sales.* / *She has no **interest in** politics.* |

#### 4. The Preposition *By*

*By* is a versatile preposition with several common uses:

| Use | Example |
|-----|---------|
| **Method/Means** | *He travels **by** plane.* / *She learned French **by** immersion.* / *We paid **by** credit card.* |
| **Agent (Passive voice)** | *The book was written **by** a famous author.* / *The song was performed **by** the choir.* |
| **Proximity** | *She sat **by** the window.* / *The shop is **by** the park.* |
| **Deadline** (not later than) | *Submit the report **by** Monday.* / *I will finish **by** 5 PM.* |
| **Measurement (difference/increase)** | *The price increased **by** 10%.* / *He is taller **by** two inches.* |
| **Part of a fixed phrase** | *She did it **by** mistake.* / *We met **by** chance.* |

#### 5. Prepositions in Numeric and Quantifier Expressions

Prepositions frequently appear in expressions of quantity, proportion, and range.

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| *out of* | *Nine **out of** ten doctors recommend this brand.* / *Two **out of** three students passed.* |
| *in* (proportion) | *One **in** five people owns a smartphone.* |
| *up to* | *The price can go **up to** $500.* |
| *as high as / as many as* | *Temperatures reached **as high as** 40 degrees.* |
| *on average* | ***On average**, people work eight hours a day.* |

### C. Talking About Exceptions

Use these prepositions and phrases to express exceptions or exclusions.

| Phrase | Use | Example |
|--------|-----|---------|
| *except* | Excluding something | *Everyone came **except** John.* |
| *except for* | Excluding (often used with general statements) | *The room was empty **except for** a chair.* |
| *apart from* | Similar to *except* or *besides* | *Apart from the weather, the trip was great.* |
| *but* (as preposition) | Excluding (often after *no, all, any*) | *There was no one **but** me at home.* |
| *excluding* | Explicitly leaving out | *The price is $100, **excluding** tax.* |
| *excluding* vs. *including* | Opposite meanings | *The total is $50, **including** shipping.* (shipping added) vs *Excluding* (shipping not added) |

**Examples:**
- *Everyone passed the exam **except** Marco.*
- *The house was perfect **except for** the small kitchen.*
- ***Apart from** a few scratches, the car is in good condition.*
- *Nobody **but** she knew the secret.*

> **Note:** *Except* and *except for* are often interchangeable, but *except for* is preferred at the beginning of a sentence. *Except* can sometimes sound more formal.

### D. Other Usages of Prepositions

#### 1. Fixed Idiomatic Prepositional Phrases

Many common expressions use specific prepositions that do not follow logical rules. These are best learned as whole phrases.

| Phrase | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| *on purpose* | Intentionally | *I didn't break it **on purpose**; it was an accident.* |
| *by accident* | Unintentionally | *I found the answer **by accident**.* |
| *under control* | Managed properly | *The situation is now **under control**.* |
| *out of control* | Unmanageable | *The fire spread **out of control**.* |
| *in charge of* | Responsible for | *She is **in charge of** the marketing department.* |
| *in trouble* | Facing difficulty | *He is **in trouble** with his boss.* |
| *on fire* | Burning | *The building was **on fire**.* |
| *under pressure* | Stressed or forced | *She works well **under pressure**.* |
| *in pain* | Suffering physically | *He was **in pain** after the fall.* |
| *out of stock* | Not available for sale | *The item is currently **out of stock**.* |
| *on time* | Punctual | *Please arrive **on time**.* |
| *in time* | With enough time to spare | *We arrived **in time** for the ceremony.* |
| *by mistake* | Accidentally (error) | *I took your coat **by mistake**.* |

---

#### 2. Prepositions with Gerunds (-ing Forms)

When a preposition is followed by a verb, that verb must take the **-ing** form (gerund). This is a non-negotiable rule.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| *Preposition + Gerund* | *Thank you **for helping** me.* / *He left **without saying** goodbye.* |
| *Instead of + Gerund* | *She stayed home **instead of going** to the party.* |
| *In spite of / Despite + Gerund* | ***In spite of feeling** tired, he finished the race.* |
| *Before / After + Gerund* | *Wash your hands **before eating**.* / *He called **after arriving**.* |
| *Insist on + Gerund* | *She insisted **on paying** for dinner.* |
| *Apologize for + Gerund* | *He apologized **for being** late.* |

---

#### 3. Prepositions in Questions and Relative Clauses

Prepositions often move to the end of the sentence in questions and informal relative clauses.

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Question (informal)** | *Who did you go **with**?* / *What are you looking **at**?* / *Where are you from?* |
| **Question (formal)** | ***With whom** did you go?* / ***At what** are you looking?* |
| **Relative clause (informal)** | *The person I spoke **to** was very helpful.* |
| **Relative clause (formal)** | *The person **to whom** I spoke was very helpful.* |

---

#### 4. Ending a Sentence with a Preposition (Stranding)

A traditional grammar rule states that a sentence should not end with a preposition. In modern English, however, this is **not an error** in most contexts. The choice depends on register (formality).

| Register | Structure | Example |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Informal / Neutral** | Preposition at the end | *This is the book I was looking **for**.* / *Who did you talk **to**?* |
| **Formal** | Preposition before relative pronoun | *This is the book **for which** I was looking.* / ***To whom** did you talk?* |

**Guideline:** In everyday speech and writing, ending a preposition is natural and common. In extremely formal or academic writing, moving the preposition forward may be preferred.

### E. Verbs with No Preposition (Common Errors)

Some verbs in English are frequently followed directly by an object **without** a preposition. Learners often incorrectly add a preposition due to interference from their native language.

| Correct (No Preposition) | Incorrect (Do Not Use) | Example |
|--------------------------|------------------------|---------|
| *discuss* | ~~discuss about~~ | *We **discussed** the problem.* (Not ~~discussed about~~) |
| *enter* (a place) | ~~enter into~~ (a place) | *She **entered** the room.* (Not ~~entered into~~) |
| *resemble* | ~~resemble to~~ | *He **resembles** his father.* (Not ~~resembles to~~) |
| *lack* (verb) | ~~lack of~~ (as a verb) | *They **lack** experience.* (Not ~~lack of~~) |
| *request* | ~~request for~~ | *He **requested** a refund.* (Not ~~requested for~~) |
| *marry* (someone) | ~~marry with / to~~ | *She **married** John.* (Not ~~married with~~) |
| *approach* | ~~approach to~~ (as a verb) | *He **approached** the building.* (Not ~~approached to~~) |
| *contact* | ~~contact with~~ (as a verb) | *Please **contact** the office.* (Not ~~contact with~~) |

> **Note:** *Lack of* is correct as a noun: *The **lack of** resources is a problem.*
>
> **Note:** *Married to* is correct in the passive/adjectival form: *She is **married to** John.*

### F. Prepositions vs. Conjunctions (Key Distinction)

**They are not the same.** While both are connecting words, they function very differently in a sentence.

#### Quick Comparison

| Feature | **Conjunction** | **Preposition** |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| **What it connects** | Clauses, phrases, or words of equal rank | A noun/pronoun to another word in the sentence |
| **Followed by** | A clause (subject + verb) | A noun, pronoun, or gerund (-ing form) |
| **Example** | *I stayed home **because** I was sick.* (clause: *I was sick*) | *I stayed home **because of** my sickness.* (noun: *my sickness*) |

#### The Same Word? Yes - But Different Functions

Some words can be **both** conjunctions and prepositions, depending on what follows them.

| Word | As a Conjunction (followed by clause) | As a Preposition (followed by noun/pronoun) |
|------|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| ***after*** | *I left **after** he arrived.* | *I left **after** dinner.* |
| ***before*** | *I left **before** he arrived.* | *I left **before** dinner.* |
| ***since*** | *I've been happy **since** I moved.* | *I've been happy **since** Monday.* |
| ***until*** | *Wait **until** I return.* | *Wait **until** midnight.* |

#### Quick Test

Ask yourself: **What follows the word?**

| If it's... | Then it's a... | Example |
|------------|----------------|---------|
| **Subject + Verb** (clause) | **Conjunction** | *because **I was tired*** |
| **Noun / Pronoun / Gerund** | **Preposition** | *because of **my tiredness*** |

### H. Common Errors to Avoid

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| *I depends **of** you.* | *I depend **on** you.* | Specific verb + preposition combinations |
| *She is interested **for** art.* | *She is interested **in** art.* | *Interested in* (not *for*) |
| *We arrived **to** the station.* | *We arrived **at** the station.* | *Arrive at* (place) / *arrive in* (city/country) |
| *He is good **in** math.* | *He is good **at** math.* | *Good at* (skill) |
| *I will meet you **in** Monday.* | *I will meet you **on** Monday.* | *On* for days; *in* for months/years |
| *She is afraid **from** spiders.* | *She is afraid **of** spiders.* | *Afraid of* (not *from*) |
| *It depends **about** the weather.* | *It depends **on** the weather.* | *Depends on* |
| *I agree **with** his proposal.* | *I agree **to** his proposal.* or *I agree **with** him.* | *Agree to* (plan/proposal); *agree with* (person) |
| *I'm married **with** John.* | *I'm married **to** John.* | *Married to* (not *with*) |
| *We discussed **about** the problem.* | *We discussed the problem.* | *Discuss* takes no preposition |
| *She entered **into** the room.* | *She entered the room.* | *Enter* takes no preposition (with place) |
| *He lacks **of** confidence.* | *He lacks confidence.* | *Lack* as verb takes no preposition |
| *Where are you **at**?* (informal) | *Where are you?* | Avoid ending with *at* in formal English |
