## Appendix F. Irregular Verbs

This appendix lists the most common irregular verbs in English. Unlike regular verbs (which add *-ed* for past forms), irregular verbs have unique past simple and past participle forms that must be memorized.

> **Note**:  
> - "Get" has two past participles: *got* (common in British English) and *gotten* (common in American English).  
> - "Lie" (to recline) -> past: *lay*, past participle: *lain* (not to be confused with "lie" meaning to tell a falsehood, which is *lied*).  
> - "Hang" (to suspend) -> past: *hung*, past participle: *hung*; "hang" (to execute) -> past: *hanged*, past participle: *hanged* (rare, formal).

> **Related Section:** See *[Section 2. Regular and Irregular Verbs](./regular-irregular-verbs.md)* for detailed explanations and patterns.

### D. Special Notes on Confusing Verbs

| Verb Pair | Difference | Past Simple | Past Participle |
|-----------|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **lie vs. lay** | *lie* = to recline (no object) | lay | lain |
| | *lay* = to put/place (needs object) | laid | laid |
| **hang vs. hang** | *hang* = to suspend | hung | hung |
| | *hang* = to execute | hanged | hanged |
| **see vs. saw** | *see* = to perceive | saw | seen |
| | *saw* = to cut (regular) | sawed | sawed / sawn |

> **Memory Aid for Lie vs. Lay:**
> - *Lie* (recline): *I **lie** down today. Yesterday I **lay** down. I have **lain** down many times.*
> - *Lay* (place): *I **lay** the book down today. Yesterday I **laid** it down. I have **laid** it down before.*

### F. Tips for Memorizing Irregular Verbs

| Strategy | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Learn in patterns** | Group by similarity (e.g., *sing/sang/sung, ring/rang/rung*) |
| **Use them in sentences** | *Yesterday I **drove** to work. I have **driven** many times.* |
| **Practice with flashcards** | Write infinitive on front; past forms on back |
| **Listen for them** | Notice irregular verbs in songs, movies, and conversations |
| **Focus on common ones first** | Master the top 20 before learning rare ones |
| **Say them aloud** | Repeat: *go-went-gone, go-went-gone, go-went-gone* |

### G. Common Errors to Avoid

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| *I have went home.* | *I have **gone** home.* |
| *She has did her homework.* | *She has **done** her homework.* |
| *He was took to the hospital.* | *He was **taken** to the hospital.* |
| *I seen that movie.* | *I **saw** that movie.* (past simple) / *I have **seen** that movie.* (present perfect) |
| *They have drank all the water.* | *They have **drunk** all the water.* |
| *She has ran every day.* | *She has **run** every day.* |
| *I should have went.* | *I should have **gone**.* |
| *He has broke the window.* | *He has **broken** the window.* |
| *The bell has rang.* | *The bell has **rung**.* |
| *I have chose the blue one.* | *I have **chosen** the blue one.* |
