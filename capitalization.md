## 1.4 Capitalization

Capitalization is the practice of writing a word with its first letter in uppercase (A, B, C) and the remaining letters in lowercase (a, b, c). Correct capitalization is essential for clear and professional writing. It signals the beginning of sentences, identifies proper nouns, and provides visual cues for readers.

> **Related Sections:** See *[Section 1.2 Punctuation](./punctuation.md)* for sentence endings and *[Section 15. Articles and Nouns](./articles-nouns.md)* for proper nouns.

---

### A. Basic Rules of Capitalization

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Capitalize the first word of every sentence** | ***T**he sun rises in the east. **I**t is a beautiful day.* |
| **Capitalize the pronoun *I*** | *My friend and **I** went to the park.* |
| **Capitalize proper nouns** (specific names) | *She lives in **Paris**. Her name is **Maria**.* |
| **Capitalize days of the week, months, and holidays** | *We met on **Monday**, **January** 15th, during **Christmas**.* |
| **Do NOT capitalize seasons** (unless part of a proper name) | *I love **spring** and **summer**. (but: **Spring** Semester)* |

---

### B. Capitalizing Proper Nouns

Proper nouns name specific people, places, organizations, or things. Common nouns are general and are **not** capitalized.

| Common Noun (Lowercase) | Proper Noun (Capitalized) |
|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| *a city* | *London, Tokyo, Cairo* |
| *a person* | *John Smith, Marie Curie* |
| *a country* | *Canada, Brazil, Japan* |
| *a river* | *the Nile River, the Amazon* |
| *a mountain* | *Mount Everest, Mount Fuji* |
| *a company* | *Apple, Microsoft, Toyota* |
| *a university* | *Harvard University, Oxford University* |
| *a religion* | *Christianity, Islam, Buddhism* |
| *a language* | *English, Spanish, Mandarin* |
| *a nationality* | *French, Japanese, Brazilian* |

#### Examples:
- *My **sister** (common) is named **Sarah** (proper).*
- *We visited **Paris** (proper) which is a beautiful **city** (common).*
- *She studies **history** (common) at **Boston College** (proper).*

### C. Capitalizing Titles and Headings

#### 1. People's Titles

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Capitalize titles before a name** | ***President** Biden, **Doctor** Smith, **Professor** Lee* |
| **Do NOT capitalize titles after a name or alone** | *Joe Biden is the **president**. I saw **doctor** Smith.* (informal) |
| **Capitalize family relationships when used as names** | *I love you, **Mom**. **Grandpa** is coming.* |
| **Do NOT capitalize family relationships with possessive or article** | *My **mom** is here. His **grandpa** is sick.* |

#### 2. Book, Movie, and Article Titles (Title Case)

In **title case**, capitalize major words. Do NOT capitalize small words (articles, conjunctions, short prepositions) unless they are the first or last word.

| Capitalize | Do NOT Capitalize | Example |
|------------|-------------------|---------|
| First and last word | *a, an, the* | ***The** Catcher in **the** Rye* |
| Nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs | *and, but, or, for, nor, yet, so* | *Pride **and** Prejudice* |
| Pronouns | *of, in, to, for, with, on, at, by* (short prepositions) | *The **of** in *The Lord of the Rings* is lowercase* |
| All words of four+ letters (including prepositions) | *to* as infinitive | *What **to** Expect When You're Expecting* |

**Examples:**
- ***The** Old Man and **the** Sea* (Hemingway)
- ***To Kill a Mockingbird*** (Harper Lee)
- ***A** Tale of Two **Cities*** (Dickens)

#### 3. Headings in Academic Writing (Sentence Case)

Many style guides (APA, Chicago) use **sentence case** for headings: capitalize only the first word and proper nouns.

| Style | Example |
|-------|---------|
| **Title Case** | *The Effects of Climate Change on Coastal Cities* |
| **Sentence Case** | *The effects of climate change on coastal cities* |

---

### D. Capitalizing Places and Geographic Terms

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Capitalize specific place names** | *I live on **Main Street** in **Los Angeles**.* |
| **Capitalize compass directions only when referring to specific regions** | *We live in the **South** (region). Turn **south** (direction) at the light.* |
| **Capitalize words like *bridge, lake, mountain, ocean* only as part of a name** | *We crossed **Brooklyn Bridge**. We swam in the **lake** (common).* |
| **Capitalize countries, nationalities, and languages** | *She is **French**. She speaks **French**. She lives in **France**.* |

**Examples:**
- *I live in the **Northeast** (region).*
- *Drive **north** for two miles (direction).*
- *The **Middle East** (region) is known for its history.*
- *Go **east** on this road (direction).*

---

### E. Capitalizing Days, Months, Holidays, and Events

| Capitalize | Do NOT Capitalize | Example |
|------------|-------------------|---------|
| Days of the week | Seasons | *I have class on **Monday** in the **fall**.* |
| Months | Centuries (as words) | *My birthday is **December** 25th in the **twenty-first century**.* |
| Holidays | Decades (as words) | *We celebrated **Thanksgiving** in the **nineties**.* |
| Historical periods/events | | *The **Renaissance** was a cultural movement.* |

**Examples:**
- *We met on **Thursday**, **October** 12th.*
- *I love **spring** and **summer**.*
- *She was born in the **nineteen eighties** (or 1980s).*

---

### F. Capitalizing Organizations, Institutions, and Brands

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Capitalize full names of organizations** | *She works for the **United Nations**.* |
| **Capitalize brand names** | *I bought an **iPhone** and **Nike** shoes.* |
| **Do NOT capitalize common nouns that follow a proper name** | *I work at **Google** the **company**.* |
| **Do NOT capitalize *the* before an organization name (unless part of the name)** | *She works at **the** United Nations (but: **The** Ohio State University)* |

**Examples:**
- *He is a professor at **Harvard University**.*
- *She drinks **Coca-Cola**.*
- *I work for **Microsoft**.*

---

### G. Capitalizing Acronyms and Initialisms

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Acronym** | Pronounced as a word | ***NASA** (National Aeronautics and Space Administration)* |
| **Initialism** | Each letter pronounced separately | ***FBI** (Federal Bureau of Investigation), **CEO*** |

**Rules:**
- Most acronyms and initialisms are written in **all caps**
- Some common acronyms become lowercase words (e.g., *radar, laser, scuba*)
- Periods are usually omitted in modern writing

**Examples:**
- *She works for **IBM**.*
- *He got his **PhD** from **MIT**.*
- *The **UN** voted on the resolution.*

---

### H. Capitalizing Quotations

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Capitalize the first word of a complete sentence in quotes** | *She said, **"T**he meeting is at 3 PM."* |
| **Do NOT capitalize a partial quote or continuation** | *He called it **"a** complete disaster."* |
| **Do NOT capitalize after *that* in reported speech** | *She said **that** the meeting was at 3 PM.* |
| **Capitalize the first word of a quoted sentence even in the middle of a sentence** | *He asked, **"W**here are you going?" and then left.* |

**Examples:**
- *She said, **"I** will be late."*
- *He described the event as **"a** nightmare."*
- *She told me **that** she was tired.* (no capitalization)

---

### I. Capitalizing After Colons

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **American English:** Capitalize after a colon only if it introduces a complete sentence or a quotation | *Here's the truth: **S**he was right all along.* |
| **British English:** Often do not capitalize after a colon | *Here's the truth: she was right all along.* |
| **Capitalize after a colon introducing a quotation** | *The president said: **"W**e will not give up."* |
| **Do not capitalize after a colon introducing a list** | *Please bring: **a** pen, **a** notebook, and **a** calculator.* |

---

### J. Capitalizing in Letters and Emails (Salutations and Closings)

| Part | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| **Salutation (opening)** | Capitalize first word and any proper nouns | ***D**ear **Mr**. Smith, / **T**o whom it may concern,* |
| **Closing** | Capitalize only the first word | ***S**incerely, / ***Y**ours truly, / ***T**hank you,* |

**Examples:**
- ***D**ear **P**rofessor **J**ohnson,*
- ***D**ear **S**ir or **M**adam,*
- ***B**est regards,*
- ***Y**ours faithfully,*

---

### K. Capitalizing in Poetry

Traditional poetry capitalizes the first word of every line, regardless of punctuation. Modern poetry often does not.

| Traditional | Modern |
|-------------|--------|
| *The sun does rise,* | *the sun does rise,* |
| *And so do I.* | *and so do i.* |
| *But where is truth?* | *but where is truth?* |
| *Beneath the sky.* | *beneath the sky.* |

---

### L. When NOT to Capitalize (Common Mistakes)

| Incorrect (Do Not Capitalize) | Correct | Rule |
|-------------------------------|---------|------|
| *I study **History**.* | *I study **history**.* | Academic subjects are not capitalized (except languages) |
| *She is a **Doctor**.* | *She is a **doctor**.* | Titles alone are not capitalized |
| *I love **Spring**.* | *I love **spring**.* | Seasons are not capitalized |
| *We live in **the** South.* | *We live in **the** South.* (capitalize region) / *We live **south** of town.* (direction lowercase) | Compass directions as regions are capitalized; as directions are not |
| *I saw **President** Biden.* (acceptable) | *I saw **President** Biden.* or *I saw **president** Biden.* (both seen) | Title before name is usually capitalized |
| *The **Earth** is round.* | *The **earth** is round.* or ***Earth*** (varies) | *Earth* as planet is capitalized; as soil is not |
| *I go to **Church** every Sunday.* (no capitalization) | *I go to **church** every Sunday.* | *Church* is not capitalized unless part of a name (*St. Mary's Church*) |

### M. Capitalization in Special Contexts

#### 1. Legal and Government Documents

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Defined terms are often capitalized | *The **Buyer** agrees to pay the **Seller**.* |
| Government entities are capitalized | *The **Federal Bureau of Investigation** released a statement.* |

#### 2. Scientific Terms

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Capitalize genus names, not species names | ****Homo** sapiens*, *Canis **familiaris*** |
| Capitalize chemical symbols, not full names (usually) | ***Na** (sodium), ***Cl** (chlorine)* |

#### 3. Social Media and Informal Writing

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Some writers use all lowercase for casual effect | *i love this song. it's amazing.* |
| ALL CAPS indicates shouting or emphasis | *I TOLD YOU TO WAIT!* |
| Capitalization is still expected in professional contexts | Use standard rules |

### N. Quick Reference Summary

| Capitalize | Do NOT Capitalize |
|------------|-------------------|
| First word of every sentence | Seasons (*spring, summer*) |
| Pronoun *I* | Common nouns (*city, river, person*) |
| Proper nouns (specific names) | Titles after a name (*president Biden* - though often capped) |
| Days, months, holidays | Academic subjects (except languages) |
| People's titles before a name (*Dr. Smith*) | Family names with possessive (*my mom*) |
| Family names as names (*I love you, Mom*) | Compass directions (*go north*) |
| Specific regions (*the South*) | Articles in titles (*the, a, an* - unless first/last) |
| Languages and nationalities | Short prepositions in titles (*of, in, to, for*) |
| Brand names | Seasons |
| Acronyms and initialisms (*NASA, FBI*) | Centuries as words (*twenty-first century*) |
| First word of a complete quotation | After *that* in reported speech |

### O. Common Errors to Avoid

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| *i love learning english.* | ***I** love learning **English**.* |
| *She speaks french and german.* | *She speaks **French** and **German**.* |
| *We visited New york city.* | *We visited **New York City**.* |
| *My Birthday is in april.* | *My birthday is in **April**.* |
| *I study Biology and Chemistry.* | *I study **biology** and **chemistry**.* (unless course names) |
| *She said, "i am tired."* | *She said, "**I** am tired."* |
| *Dear sir, I am writing...* | *Dear **Sir**, I am writing...* |
| *The President of the United states.* | *The President of the United **States**.* |
| *I love the Spring season.* | *I love the **spring** season.* |

### P. Capitalization Styles: US vs. UK (Minor Differences)

| Context | US English | UK English |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **After colon** | Capitalize if complete sentence | Often lowercase |
| **Titles (headings)** | Title case common | Sentence case common |
| ***The* in organization names** | Often lowercase (*the United Nations*) | Same (but varies) |
| **Historical periods** | Capitalized (*the Renaissance*) | Same |
