## Appendix D. Silent Letters

Silent letters are letters that appear in the spelling of a word but are not pronounced when the word is spoken.

They can be confusing for learners of English, as spelling often doesn't directly match pronunciation.

> **Related Section:** See *Appendix B: Pronunciation* for more on English sounds and pronunciation patterns.

Here are some common reasons why silent letters exist:

**Distinguishing Homophones:** They help differentiate between words that sound alike but have different meanings and spellings (e.g., *be/bee*, *to/too/two*, *knight/night*).

**Historical Reasons:** English spelling has evolved over centuries, and some spellings reflect older pronunciations or influences from other languages (like Latin or French).

**Etymology:** They can sometimes indicate a word's origin or relationship to other words in the same language family (e.g., *sign* and *signal*).

**Pronunciation Guidance:** In some cases, a silent letter might subtly affect the pronunciation of other letters in the word or indicate stress patterns.

**Silent A**
Often appears in specific words, particularly after `a` in combinations like `-ea-` or `-ai-`.
- **Bread** (pronounced /bred/)
- **Breakfast** (pronounced /'brekfast/)
- **Head** (pronounced /hed/)
- **Steak** (pronounced /ste…™k/)

**Silent B**
Frequently silent before `m` at the end of a word, or before `t`.
- **Bomb** (pronounced /b…ím/)
- **Climb** (pronounced /kla…™m/)
- **Doubt** (pronounced /da ät/)
- **Debt** (pronounced /det/)
- **Subtle** (pronounced /'s Ćtl/)

**Silent C**
Often silent in consonant clusters like `-sc-`, `-sce-`, `-sci-`.
- **Scene** (pronounced /siňźn/)
- **Scissors** (pronounced /'s…™z…ôrz/)
- **Muscle** (pronounced /'m Ćsl/)
- **Acquire** (pronounced /…ôňąkwa…™…ôr/)

**Silent D**
Usually silent in consonant clusters like `-dg-`, `-dj-`, `-nd-`, or in `-nd't`.
- **Edge** (pronounced /ed í/)
- **Wednesday** (pronounced /'wenzde…™/)
- **Handsome** (pronounced /'h√¶ns…ôm/)
- **Didn't** (pronounced /'d…™dnt/)

**Silent E**
A very common silent letter, usually found at the end of words. It often makes the preceding vowel have a long sound.
- **Name** (pronounced /ne…™m/)
- **Like** (pronounced /la…™k/)
- **Hope** (pronounced /h…ô äp/)

**Silent G**
Often silent before `n` at the beginning of a word, or in `-gh-` combinations.
- **Gnome** (pronounced /n…ô äm/)
- **Gnash** (pronounced /n√¶ É/)
- **Light** (pronounced /la…™t/)
- **High** (pronounced /ha…™/)
- **Drought** (pronounced /dra ät/)

**Silent H**
Can be silent at the beginning of words (especially after `w`) or within words.
- **What** (pronounced /w…ít/)
- **When** (pronounced /wen/)
- **Hour** (pronounced /a ä…ôr/)
- **Ghost** (pronounced /…°…ô äst/)
- **Rhythm** (pronounced /'r…™√į…ôm/)

**Silent K**
Always silent before `n` at the beginning of a word.
- **Know** (pronounced /n…ô ä/)
- **Knife** (pronounced /na…™f/)
- **Knee** (pronounced /niňź/)

**Silent L**
Often silent in consonant clusters like `-alm-`, `-alf-`, `-alk-`, `-olk-`, and `-ould-`.
- **Calm** (pronounced /k…Ďňźm/)
- **Half** (pronounced /h…Ďňźf/)
- **Walk** (pronounced /w…Ēňźk/)
- **Could** (pronounced /k äd/)
- **Should** (pronounced / É äd/)

**Silent N**
Silent after `m` at the end of a word.
- **Autumn** (pronounced /'…Ēňźt…ôm/)
- **Column** (pronounced /'k…íl…ôm/)
- **Hymn** (pronounced /h…™m/)

**Silent P**
Silent at the beginning of words with `-pn-`, `-ps-`, `-pt-` (often of Greek origin) and in `-pt` combinations.
- **Pneumonia** (pronounced /njuňźňąm…ô äni…ô/)
- **Psychology** (pronounced /sa…™ňąk…íl…ôd íi/)
- **Pterodactyl** (pronounced /ňĆter…ôňąd√¶kt…™l/)
- **Receipt** (pronounced /r…™ňąsiňźt/)

**Silent S**
Can be silent in clusters like `-isl-`, `-isle-`', `-ist-`.
- **Island** (pronounced /'a…™l…ônd/)
- **Aisle** (pronounced /a…™l/)
- **Listen** (pronounced /'l…™s…ôn/)
- **Ballet** (pronounced /b√¶le…™/)

**Silent T**
Often silent in clusters like `-stle-`, `-sten-`, or in words like `often`.
- **Castle** (pronounced /'k…Ďňźsl/)
- **Whistle** (pronounced /'w…™sl/)
- **Often** (pronounced /'…íf…ôn/ or /'…íft…ôn/ - pronunciation varies, but silent 't' is common)
- **Listen** (pronounced /'l…™s…ôn/)
- **Boutique** (pronounced /buňźňątiňźk/)

**Silent U**
Can be silent after `g` and before a vowel.
- **Guess** (pronounced /…°es/)
- **Guest** (pronounced /…°est/)
- **Build** (pronounced /b…™ld/)
- **Guard** (pronounced /…°…Ďňźrd/)

**Silent W**
Silent at the beginning of words starting with `wr-`, and in words like [answer] and [sword].
- **Write** (pronounced /ra…™t/)
- **Who** (pronounced /huňź/)
- **Wrong** (pronounced /r…íŇč/)
- **Answer** (pronounced /'…Ďňźns…ôr/)
- **Sword** (pronounced /s…Ēňźrd/)

This is not an exhaustive list, but it covers many of the most common silent letters and their patterns.
