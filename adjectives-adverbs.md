## 19. Adjectives and Adverbs

**Adjectives** describe the features of a noun or pronoun. **Adverbs** modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

> **Related Sections:** See *Section 14. `-ing` and `to` Forms (Verbals)* for Participle (Participial Phrases) and *Section 16. Pronouns and Determiners* for interrogative pronouns and relative pronouns. 

---

### A. Adjectives

Adjectives provide more information about the qualities or characteristics of nouns and pronouns.

#### 1. Placement of Adjectives

| Position | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Before a noun** (most common) | *She has a **beautiful** garden.* / *We bought two **large** pizzas.* |
| **After a linking verb** (*be, seem, become, feel, look, sound, taste, smell*) | *The soup tastes **delicious**.* / *You look **happy**.* / *He is **tired**.* |
| **After a pronoun** | *Everyone **is welcome**.* |
| **After an object** (object complement) | *We found the movie **boring**.* / *She painted the room **blue**.* |

##### Adjective as Noun Modifier vs. Complement 

| Function | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Noun modifier** (attributive) | *a **red** car* |
| **Subject complement** (after linking verb) | *The car is **red**.* |
| **Object complement** | *She painted the car **red**.* |

---

#### 2. Degrees of Adjectives

Adjectives change form to compare two or more things.

##### Comparative Degree (comparing two things)

| Adjective Type | Formation | Example |
|----------------|-----------|---------|
| **Short adjectives** (one syllable, some two syllables*) | Add *-er* | *cheap -> cheaper* / *fast -> faster* / *happy -> happier* |
| **Long adjectives** (most two syllables, three or more) | Use *more* | *expensive -> more expensive* / *beautiful -> more beautiful* |
| **Irregular adjectives** | Special form | *good -> better* / *bad -> worse* / *far -> farther/further* |

**Structure with *than*:** *[Comparative Adjective] + than + [noun/pronoun]*

- *This book is cheaper **than** that one.*
- *She is more intelligent **than** her brother.*
- *Your car is much better **than** mine.* (Use *much, a lot, far, slightly, a little* before comparatives)

**Equality / Inequality:**
- *as + [adjective] + as:* *He is **as tall as** his father.*
- *not as + [adjective] + as / less + [adjective] + than:* *This is **not as difficult as** I expected.* / *The second option is **less convenient than** the first.*

##### Superlative Degree (comparing three or more things)

| Adjective Type | Formation | Example |
|----------------|-----------|---------|
| **Short adjectives** (one syllable, some two syllables*) | Add *-est* | *long -> **the longest** / fast -> **the fastest** / happy -> **the happiest*** |
| **Long adjectives** (most two syllables, three or more) | Use *most* | *enjoyable -> **the most enjoyable** / expensive -> **the most expensive*** |
| **Irregular adjectives** | Special form | *good -> **the best** / bad -> **the worst** / far -> **the farthest/furthest*** |

**Structure with *the*:** *the + [Superlative Adjective]*

- *This is **the longest** river in the world.*
- *She is **the most talented** singer in the group.*
- *That was **the best** meal I've ever had.*

---

> *Two syllable adjectives ending in `-y`, `-le`, `-er`, `-ow` take -er/-est (happy -> happier, simple -> simplest, clever -> cleverer, narrow -> narrowest)  
> Most others take more/most (famous -> more famous, careful -> most careful)  

#### 3. Predicative vs. Attributive Adjectives

Some adjectives can only go in one position.

| Type | Position | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Attributive only** (before noun only) | Before a noun | *the **main** reason* (incorrect: *The reason is main*) / *an **utter** lie* / *the **only** person* |
| **Predicative only** (after linking verb only) | After a linking verb | *The child is **asleep**.* (incorrect: *an asleep child*) / *She felt **ill**.* / *The ship is **afloat**.* |

**Common predicative-only adjectives:** *asleep, awake, alive, alone, afraid, ill, well, glad, sorry, sure, fond, aware*

**Common attributive-only adjectives:** *main, principal, mere, sheer, utter, only, eventual, elder, indoor, outdoor*

---

#### 4. Participial Adjectives (-ing vs. -ed)

Participial adjectives come from verbs and describe feelings or states.

| Ending | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **-ing** | Causes the feeling (active) | *The movie was **boring**.* (it bored me) |
| **-ed** | Receives the feeling (passive) | *I felt **bored**.* (I was bored) |

| Common Pairs |
|--------------|
| *interesting / interested* |
| *exciting / excited* |
| *surprising / surprised* |
| *frightening / frightened* |
| *tiring / tired* |
| *disappointing / disappointed* |

> **Rule of thumb:** Use *-ing* for the **cause**, *-ed* for the **effect**.

---

#### 5. Repeating Adjectives

Repeating an adjective is a stylistic device used for **emphasis**, creating stronger emotional impact, or suggesting a continuous state.

| Purpose | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Emphasis** | *It was a **long, long** journey.* / *She was **very, very** tired.* |
| **Continuous state** | *Their laughter was **high, high** in the air.* |
| **Figurative language** | *A **cold, cold** wind chilled them to the bone.* |

> **Note:** This is more common in informal speech or literary contexts than formal writing.

---

#### 6. Adjectives Functioning as Nouns

Sometimes adjectives (especially with *the*) can refer to a group of people or a concept.

| Use | Example |
|-----|---------|
| **Groups of people** (takes plural verb) | *The rich should help **the poor**.* / *We need to support **the unemployed**.* |
| **Concepts** (less common) | *We must strive for **the sublime**.* / *He only cared about **the beautiful**.* |

---

#### 5. Compound Adjectives

Formed by combining two or more words to act as a single adjective. **Hyphenate when they come *before* the noun.**

| Formation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Adjective + Participle |  *a **fast-moving** train* / *a **well-known** author* |
| Noun + Participle | *a **mouth-watering** dessert* / ***sun-tanned** skin* |
| Adjective + Adjective | *a **brand-new** car* / *a **light-blue** sky* / *a **bitter-sweet** memory* |
| Number + Noun | *a **five-year-old** boy* / *a **two-week** holiday* |

> **Hyphenation Rule:** Hyphenate before the noun (*a **state-of-the-art** computer*). Do not hyphenate after the noun (*The computer is state of the art*).

---

#### 6. Gradable vs. Non-Gradable (Absolute) Adjectives

| Type | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Gradable** | Qualities that exist in degrees; can be modified by *very, extremely, slightly*; have comparative/superlative forms | *hot/cold, big/small, happy/sad, good/bad* |
| **Non-Gradable (Absolute)** | Absolute qualities; cannot be modified by *very*; no comparative forms | *dead, unique, pregnant, impossible, true/false, boiling/freezing* |

> **Note:** *Very unique* is considered incorrect in standard English and cannot be used with absolute adjectives; more:

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| *completely unique* | *very unique* |
| *almost impossible* | *very impossible* |
| *perfectly correct* | *very correct* |
| *entirely dead* | *very dead* |

---

#### 7. Adjectives with Prepositions (Collocations)

Many adjectives are followed by specific prepositions.

| Adjective + Preposition | Example |
|------------------------|---------|
| *good at / bad at* | *She is **good at** math.* |
| *interested in* | *He is **interested in** art.* |
| *afraid of* | *I am **afraid of** spiders.* |
| *different from* (US: *than*) | *This is **different from** that.* |
| *responsible for* | *Who is **responsible for** this?* |
| *married to* | *She is **married to** John.* |
| *similar to* | *This is **similar to** mine.* |
| *fond of* | *I am **fond of** chocolate.* |
| *aware of* | *Are you **aware of** the risks?* |
| *proud of* | *She is **proud of** her son.* |
| *angry with* (person) | *I am **angry with** him.* |
| *angry about* (situation) | *I am **angry about** the delay.* |

---

#### 8. Double Comparatives and Superlatives (Common Error)

Using two comparative forms together is **incorrect** in standard English.

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| *This book is **more better**...* | *This book is **better**...* / *...**much better**...* |
| *She is **more taller**...* | *She is **taller**...* / *...**a lot taller**...* |
| *This is the **most best** option.* | *This is the **best** option.* |

### B. Adverbs

Adverbs describe or modify verbs, adjectives, other adverbs, or entire clauses.

#### 1. Formation of Adverbs

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Add *-ly* to adjective | *quick -> quickly* / *beautiful -> beautifully* / *careful -> carefully* |
| Adjective ending in *-y* -> change *y* to *i* + *-ly* | *happy -> happily* / *easy -> easily* |
| Adjectives ending in *-able, -ible, -le, -ic* -> add *-y* or *-ally* | *comfortable -> comfortably* / *terrible -> terribly* / *gentle -> gently* / *basic -> basically* / *specific -> specifically* |

**Common irregular adverbs:**
- *good -> well*
- *fast -> fast* (same form)
- *late -> late* (same form)
- *hard -> hard* (diligently) - *Hardly* means "scarcely" or "almost not"

> **Important distinction:** *He works **hard**.* (diligently) vs. *He **hardly** works.* (almost doesn't work)

---

#### 2. Adverbs of Manner (answer "How?")

| Example |
|---------|
| *He speaks **quickly**.* |
| *She sang **beautifully**.* |
| *They played **well**.* |
| *He did **fast**.* |
| *The train arrived **late**.* |

---

#### 3. Adverbs of Frequency (answer "How often?")

| Adverb | Approximate Frequency | Example |
|--------|----------------------|---------|
| *always* | 100% | *I **always** brush my teeth.* |
| *usually / generally* | ~90% | *We **usually** have dinner at 7 PM.* |
| *often / frequently* | ~70% | *The bus **often** gets stuck in traffic.* |
| *sometimes / occasionally* | ~50% | *I **sometimes** go for a walk.* |
| *rarely / seldom / hardly ever* | ~10% | *He **rarely** eats fast food.* |
| *never* | 0% | *She **never** misses a deadline.* |

**Placement:**
- Before the main verb: *She **often** visits us.*
- After the verb *be*: *He **is always** late.*
- Between auxiliary and main verb: *They **have never tried** sushi.*

#### 10. Interrogative Adverbs (used to ask questions)

| Adverb | Question | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| *when* | Asks about time | ***When** will you arrive?* |
| *where* | Asks about place | ***Where** did you put my phone?* |
| *why* | Asks about reason | ***Why** are you late?* |
| *how* | Asks about manner | ***How** did you do that?* |

##### Interrogative Pronouns vs. Interrogative Adverbs

| | **Interrogative Pronouns** | **Interrogative Adverbs** |
|---|---|---|
| **Function** | Replace a **noun** (person, thing, idea) | Modify a **verb** (ask about time, place, reason, manner) |
| **What they ask for** | The identity of someone/something | Circumstances (when, where, why, how) |
| **Answer is a** | Noun / noun phrase | Adverb / prepositional phrase |

| Word | As Interrogative **Pronoun** | As Interrogative **Adverb** |
|------|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| *what* | *What is your name?* (answer: a noun -> "John") |  Not an adverb |
| *which* | *Which do you prefer?* (answer: a noun -> "the red one") |  Not an adverb |
| *who* | *Who called?* (answer: a noun -> "Maria") |  Not an adverb |
| *whom* | *Whom did you see?* (answer: a noun -> "my teacher") |  Not an adverb |
| *whose* | *Whose is this?* (answer: a noun -> "Julia's") |  Not an adverb |
| *when* |  Not a pronoun | *When will you arrive?* (answer: time -> "tomorrow") |
| *where* |  Not a pronoun | *Where do you live?* (answer: place -> "in Paris") |
| *why* |  Not a pronoun | *Why are you crying?* (answer: reason -> "because I'm sad") |
| *how* |  Not a pronoun | *How did you do that?* (answer: manner -> "carefully") |

- Interrogative Pronoun (replaces a noun)
    - ***Who** is at the door?* -> Answer: *John* (noun)
    - ***What** is that?* -> Answer: *A spider* (noun phrase)
    - ***Which** do you want?* -> Answer: *The blue one* (noun phrase)

- Interrogative Adverb (modifies a verb)
    - ***When** will you come?* -> Answer: *Tomorrow* (time adverb)
    - ***Where** are my keys?* -> Answer: *On the table* (place prepositional phrase)
    - ***Why** did you lie?* -> Answer: *Because I was scared* (reason clause)
    - ***How** does this work?* -> Answer: *Easily* (manner adverb)

###### Memory Trick

| | Interrogative **Pronoun** | Interrogative **Adverb** |
|---|---|---|
| **Typical words** | *who, whom, whose, which, what* | *when, where, why, how* |
| **Can answer with** | A person or thing | A time, place, reason, or manner |
| **Test** | Replace with *he/she/it/they* (pronoun) | Replace with *then/there/because/quickly* (adverb) |

- **Example tests:**
    - *What is that?* -> *It is a pen.* (pronoun works)
    - *When will you go?* -> *Then.* (adverb works)

#### 11. Relative Adverbs (connect a clause to a noun)

| Adverb | Example |
|--------|---------|
| *when* | *I remember the day **when** we first met.* |
| *where* | *That's the restaurant **where** I had my first date.* |
| *why* | *Can you explain the reason **why** you were absent?* |

##### Relative Pronouns vs Relative Adverbs

| | **Relative Pronouns** | **Relative Adverbs** |
|---|---|---|
| **Function** | Replace a **noun** (person/thing) | Replace a **preposition + noun** (time/place/reason) |
| **What they introduce** | Adjective clause modifying a noun | Adjective clause modifying a noun (of time, place, or reason) |
| **Answer the question** | *Who? Which? What?* | *When? Where? Why?* |
| **Words** | *who, whom, which, that, whose* | *when, where, why* |

###### Relative Adverbs (refer to a time, place, or reason)

| Adverb | Refers to | Replaces | Example |
|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| *when* | time | *in/on/at which* | *I remember the day **when** we met.* (= on which) |
| *where* | place | *in/at/on which* | *That's the restaurant **where** I ate.* (= at which) |
| *why* | reason | *for which* | *Tell me the reason **why** you left.* (= for which) |

**In the clause, the relative adverb acts as an:**
- **Adverb of time:** *the day **when** we met* -> *when* modifies *met* (telling when)
- **Adverb of place:** *the house **where** I grew up* -> *where* modifies *grew up* (telling where)
- **Adverb of reason:** *the reason **why** I called* -> *why* modifies *called* (telling why)

###### Relative Pronouns (refer to a noun)

| Pronoun | Refers to | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| *who* | people (subject) | *The man **who** called is my uncle.* |
| *whom* | people (object) | *The woman **whom** I met is a doctor.* |
| *which* | things/animals | *The book, **which** I read, was great.* |
| *that* | people or things | *The car **that** I bought is red.* |
| *whose* | possession | *The student **whose** phone rang was embarrassed.* |

**In the clause, the relative pronoun acts as a:**
- **Subject:** *The man **who** called me* -> *who* = subject of *called*
- **Object:** *The book **that** I read* -> *that* = object of *read*
- **Possessive:** *The girl **whose** bag is red* -> *whose* = shows possession

###### Side-by-Side Examples

| Clause Type | Relative Pronoun | Relative Adverb |
|-------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Time** | Not used | *I'll never forget the day **when** we first met.* |
| **Place** | Not used | *This is the village **where** I was born.* |
| **Reason** | Not used | *That's the reason **why** I'm late.* |
| **Person** | *The woman **who** helped me is kind.* | Not used |
| **Thing** | *The book **that** I borrowed is great.* | Not used |
| **Possession** | *The man **whose** car was stolen called the police.* | Not used |

> Special Note: *That* as a Relative Adverb

In informal English, *that* can replace *when, where, or why*:

| Formal (Relative Adverb) | Informal (Relative *that*) |
|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| *the day **when** we met* | *the day **that** we met* |
| *the place **where** I live* | *the place **that** I live* |
| *the reason **why** I came* | *the reason **that** I came* |

> In these cases, *that* is still functioning as a relative adverb (not a pronoun), but many grammars simply call it a relative pronoun used adverbially.

###### Memory Trick

| | Relative **Pronoun** | Relative **Adverb** |
|---|---|---|
| **Replaces** | A noun (person/thing) | A time, place, or reason |
| **Can be replaced by** | *he/she/it/they* | *then/there/because* |
| **Example replacement** | *the man **who** called* -> *he called* | *the day **when** we met* -> *we met **then*** |

#### 12. Placement of Adverbs

| Position | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Before the main verb | *She **carefully** opened the box.* |
| After the verb (no object) | *He speaks **slowly**.* |
| After the object | *She opened the box **carefully**.* |
| Between auxiliary and main verb | *He **has never seen** such a thing.* / *They **will probably be** late.* |
| Before an adjective | *It was an **extremely hot** day.* |
| Before another adverb | *He drove **incredibly fast**.* |

#### 13. Intensifiers and Mitigators (Adverbs of Degree)

| Type | Adverbs | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Intensifiers** (stronger) | *so, too, quite, very, really, extremely, incredibly* | *It's **so** cold!* / *This coffee is **too** hot to drink.* / *The exam was **extremely** difficult.* |
| **Mitigators** (weaker) | *enough, quite, pretty, rather, fairly* | *Is he tall **enough**?* / *The movie was **pretty** good.* / *The test was **fairly** easy.* |

**Special notes:**
- *too* = "more than enough" / "excessively" (often followed by an infinitive: *too hot **to drink***)
- *enough* in comes **after** the adjective/adverb: *tall **enough***, *fast **enough***. (and comes before nouns)
- *quite* has two meanings (not just a mitigator):

| Quite Meaning | Strength | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Somewhat / fairly** (mitigator - weaker) | ~50% | *The movie was **quite good**.* (British English: "pretty good") |
| **Completely / absolutely** (intensifier - stronger) | 100% | *You're **quite right**.* / *That's **quite impossible**.* |

- *Pretty* as an adverb meaning "fairly", but note it's **informal/colloquial**:
    - *The test was **pretty** easy.* (fine in speech, less common in formal writing)

#### 14. Common Adverbs Examples

| Adverb | Use | Example |
|--------|-----|---------|
| *so / such* | Express result or degree | *He was **so** tired **that** he fell asleep.* / *It was **such** a difficult test **that** no one passed.* |
| *still* | Continuation of an action/state | *Are you **still** working?* / *He **still** doesn't understand.* |
| *yet* | "up until now" (negatives/questions, often with present perfect) | *Have you finished **yet**?* / *I haven't seen him **yet**.* |
| *already* | Happened earlier than expected (positive statements) | *They have **already** arrived.* / *I've **already** eaten.* |
| *even* | Emphasis (surprising or unexpected) | ***Even** a child can do it.* / *He didn't **even** say goodbye.* |

#### 15. Interrogative vs. Relative: Pronouns & Adverbs

**Interrogative pronouns** and **adverbs** ask questions (replacing nouns or modifying verbs), while **relative pronouns** and **adverbs** introduce adjective clauses (referring back to a noun or a time/place/reason).

| Aspect | Interrogative Pronouns | Interrogative Adverbs | Relative Pronouns | Relative Adverbs |
|--------|------------------------|----------------------|--------------------|------------------|
| **Part of Speech** | Pronoun | Adverb | Pronoun | Adverb |
| **Function** | Ask a question | Ask a question | Introduce an adjective clause | Introduce an adjective clause |
| **Sentence ends with** | `?` | `?` | `.` or `!` | `.` or `!` |
| **Has an antecedent?** | ❌ No | ❌ No | ✅ Yes (refers to a noun before) | ✅ Yes (refers to time/place/reason) |
| **Common words** | *who, whom, whose, what, which* | *when, where, why, how* | *who, whom, whose, which, that* | *when, where, why* |

---

##### Pronouns

| Word | Interrogative (asks a question) | Relative (introduces adjective clause) |
|------|--------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| *who* | ***Who** called?* | *The man **who** called is John.* |
| *whom* | ***Whom** did you see?* | *The teacher **whom** I saw is kind.* |
| *whose* | ***Whose** is this?* | *Maria, **whose** phone rang, is here.* |
| *which* | ***Which** is faster?* | *The red car, **which** is faster, is mine.* |
| *what* | ***What** do you want?* | ⚠️ *I saw **what** you did.* (fused relative - special case) |

> **Note:** *What* is not a standard relative pronoun. Use *that* or *which* for adjective clauses.

---

##### Adverbs

| Word | Interrogative (asks a question) | Relative (introduces adjective clause) |
|------|--------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| *when* | ***When** will you arrive?* | *I remember the day **when** we met.* |
| *where* | ***Where** do you live?* | *That's the house **where** I grew up.* |
| *why* | ***Why** are you late?* | *Tell me the reason **why** you left.* |
| *how* | ***How** did you do that?* | ❌ Not used (say *the way **in which / that***) |

> ❌ *When, where, why* are never pronouns. They are always adverbs.
>
> ❌ *How* is only an interrogative adverb (never relative).

---

##### *That* (Special Case)

| Interrogative | Relative Pronoun | Relative Adverb (informal) |
|--------------|------------------|---------------------------|
| ❌ Not used | *The car **that** I bought is red.* | *The day **that** we met was rainy.* (replaces *when*) |

> **Note:** *That* can function as a **relative pronoun** (referring to people/things) or informally as a **relative adverb** (replacing *when, where, why*).

---

##### Quick Reference

| | Interrogative Pronouns | Interrogative Adverbs | Relative Pronouns | Relative Adverbs |
|---|----------------------|----------------------|--------------------|------------------|
| **Purpose** | Ask a question (replace noun) | Ask a question (modify verb) | Describe a noun | Describe time/place/reason |
| **Ending** | `?` | `?` | `.` or `!` | `.` or `!` |
| **Antecedent?** | No | No | Yes | Yes |
| **Words** | *who, whom, whose, what, which* | *when, where, why, how* | *who, whom, whose, which, that* | *when, where, why* |

##### Memory Trick (One Simple Flowchart)

| Ask yourself | -> Then it is... |
|--------------|------------------|
| Does it ask a question (end with `?`)? | **Interrogative** |
| -> Does it replace a noun? | -> **Interrogative Pronoun** |
| -> Does it modify a verb? | -> **Interrogative Adverb** |
| Does it introduce a clause (no `?`)? | **Relative** |
| -> Does it replace a noun? | -> **Relative Pronoun** |
| -> Does it refer to time/place/reason? | -> **Relative Adverb** |

---

##### Common Errors to Avoid

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| *The thing **what** I saw* | *The thing **that/which** I saw* |
| *The way **how** he did it* | *The way **that/in which** he did it* |
| *I know the reason **why** he left* | ✅ Correct (relative adverb) |
| ***Why** did he leave?* | ✅ Correct (interrogative adverb) |

### C. Adjective vs. Adverb Confusion

| Incorrect | Correct | Rule |
|-----------|---------|------|
| *She drives **careful**.* | *She drives **carefully**.* | Modifying a verb -> use adverb |
| *I feel **badly**.* | *I feel **bad**.* | After linking verbs -> use adjective |
| *He ran **quick**.* | *He ran **quickly**.* | Modifying action verb -> use adverb |

> **Note:** *Feel good* (emotionally) vs. *feel well* (healthy) - both are correct but different meanings.

### E. Quick Reference Summary

| Part of Speech | Function | Example |
|----------------|----------|---------|
| **Adjective** | Modifies a noun/pronoun | *She is **happy**.* / *A **beautiful** day.* |
| **Adverb** | Modifies verb, adjective, or another adverb | *She sings **beautifully**.* / ***Very** happy.* |

| Degree | Adjective Example | Adverb Example |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| Positive | *fast* | *fast* |
| Comparative | *faster* (than) | *faster* (than) |
| Superlative | *(the) fastest* | *(the) fastest* |
