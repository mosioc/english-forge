# 1.6 Sentence Types by Function

Every English sentence serves one of four communicative purposes. These are called **sentence functions** or **sentence types**.

---

## A. Declarative Sentences (Statements)

**Function:** To give information, state facts, or express opinions.

**Structure:** Subject + Verb (+ Object/Complement)

| Example | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| *The sun rises in the east.* | Stating a fact |
| *She works at a hospital.* | Giving information |
| *I think this is a bad idea.* | Expressing an opinion |
| *They have never been to Paris.* | Negative statement |

**Word Order:** Standard SVO (Subject-Verb-Object).

**Punctuation:** Period (full stop).

**Negation:** Add *not* after the auxiliary verb, or use *do-support* in simple tenses.
- *She is coming.* -> *She is **not** coming.*
- *They like coffee.* -> *They **do not** like coffee.*

---

## B. Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

**Function:** To ask for information or confirmation.

**Structure:** (Question Word) + Auxiliary/Modal + Subject + Main Verb (+ ...)?

### 1. Yes/No Questions

Expect *yes* or *no* as an answer.

| Statement | Yes/No Question |
|-----------|-----------------|
| *She is tired.* | *Is she tired?* |
| *They have finished.* | *Have they finished?* |
| *He plays piano.* | *Does he play piano?* |

### 2. Wh-Questions

Begin with a question word and ask for specific information.

| Question Word | Asks About | Example |
|---------------|------------|---------|
| *Who* | Person | *Who called you?* |
| *What* | Thing / Idea | *What do you want?* |
| *Where* | Place | *Where does she live?* |
| *When* | Time | *When did they arrive?* |
| *Why* | Reason | *Why are you late?* |
| *How* | Manner / Method | *How do you spell that?* |
| *Which* | Choice | *Which book is yours?* |
| *Whose* | Possession | *Whose bag is this?* |

### 3. Alternative Questions (Choice)

Offer options using *or*.

- *Would you like tea **or** coffee?*
- *Is she flying **or** driving?*

### 4. Tag Questions

A statement followed by a short question "tag."

- *You're coming, **aren't you**?*
- *She doesn't eat meat, **does she**?*

> **See also:** *[Section 13. Questions (Full Treatment)](./questions.md)*

**Punctuation:** Question mark (?).

---

## C. Imperative Sentences (Commands / Requests)

**Function:** To tell someone to do (or not do) something. Includes commands, requests, instructions, invitations, and warnings.

**Structure:** Base verb (no subject). The implied subject is always *you*.

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Command | *Sit down.* |
| Request | *Please pass the salt.* |
| Instruction | *Turn left at the traffic light.* |
| Invitation | *Come in!* |
| Warning | *Watch out!* |
| Negative Command | *Don't touch that.* |

**Notes:**
- The subject *you* is usually omitted but can be included for emphasis or contrast: *You stay here. You come with me.*
- *Let's* + base verb is used for suggestions involving the speaker: *Let's go. Let's not argue.*

**Punctuation:** Period (.) for neutral commands. Exclamation mark (!) for strong or urgent commands.

---

## D. Exclamative Sentences (Exclamations)

**Function:** To express strong emotion, surprise, admiration, or emphasis.

**Structure:** Two main patterns using *What* or *How*.

### 1. With *What* (+ Noun Phrase)

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| *What + a/an + adjective + singular noun* | *What a beautiful day!* |
| *What + adjective + plural noun* | *What gorgeous flowers!* |
| *What + adjective + uncountable noun* | *What terrible weather!* |

### 2. With *How* (+ Adjective/Adverb)

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| *How + adjective* | *How wonderful!* |
| *How + adjective/adverb + subject + verb* | *How quickly she runs!* |
| *How + subject + verb* | *How they've grown!* |

### 3. Other Exclamation Patterns

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| With *so* / *such* | *You're so kind! / That's such good news!* |
| One-word exclamation | *Wow! / Amazing! / No!* |
| Rhetorical question | *Isn't that gorgeous!* |

> **See also:** *[Section 13.2 Exclamations and Interjections](./exclamations-interjections.md)*

**Punctuation:** Exclamation mark (!). Note: Overuse of exclamation marks is considered poor style in formal writing.

---

## E. Summary Table

| Sentence Type | Function | Typical Punctuation | Typical Word Order |
|---------------|----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Declarative** | Give information | . (period) | Subject-Verb-Object |
| **Interrogative** | Ask for information | ? (question mark) | Auxiliary-Subject-Verb |
| **Imperative** | Give command/request | . or ! | Verb (no subject) |
| **Exclamative** | Express emotion | ! (exclamation mark) | *What/How* + phrase |

---

## F. Quick Reference: Identifying Sentence Types

1. Does it ask something? -> **Interrogative** (?)
2. Does it command or request? -> **Imperative** (base verb, no visible subject)
3. Does it start with *What* or *How* and express strong feeling? -> **Exclamative** (!)
4. Otherwise -> **Declarative** (.)

---

## G. Common Errors to Avoid

| Error | Correction | Why |
|-------|------------|-----|
| *Where you are going?* | *Where **are** you going?* | Questions require auxiliary before subject |
| *Please to sit down.* | *Please sit down.* | Imperative uses base verb, never infinitive |
| *What a beautiful!* | *What a beautiful **day/place/view**!* | *What* must be followed by a noun phrase |
| *How beautiful day!* | *What a beautiful day!* | *How* modifies adjectives/adverbs directly, not noun phrases |
